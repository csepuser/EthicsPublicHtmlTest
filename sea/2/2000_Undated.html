IEEE Computer Society and ACM Software Engineering Coordinating Committee

Annual Report - 2000

Prepared by Dennis Frailey, co-chair and Leonard Tripp, chair

Introduction

The committee was formed in the fourth quarter of 1998 to replace the ad hoc Joint IEEE Computer Society and ACM Steering Committee for the Establishment of Software Engineering as a Profession.

This report is organized into the following elements: actions taken to form the committee, key events that occurred in 2000 that had impact on the future of the committee, committee actions, and results achieved
by committee projects.

Committee Formation

The membership of first Executive Committee are: Mark Ardis, Doris Carver, Dennis Frailey, Linda Northrup (resignation pending), Karl Reed, and Leonard Tripp.

Key Events

March 2000. Committee held annual meeting in Austin, Texas.
June 2000. ACM Council voted to withdraw from the SWEcc. Actions

The SWEcc approves the proposed logo for the software engineering code of ethics.
The SWEcc approves the copyright of the logo as a committee asset.

Proiect Results

The third review of Guide to Software Engineering Body of Knowledge was completed in July 2000. Over 3500 comments generated. A plan was prepared to resolve review comments.

Annual Survey of International Software Engineering Academic Programs: Progress Report Number 2,
November 2000.
