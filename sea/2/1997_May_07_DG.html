IFIP SIG9.2.2 `IFIP Framework on Ethics'

Abstract from: Minutes of the Corfu meeting Wednesday, May 7, 1997

9.a. IEEE Draft Software Engineering Code of Ethics (http://computer.org/tab/seprof/code.htm)

The effort of the IEEE task force in generating this code was very much appreciated.

A number of rather direct, quite general observations were first made by the group:

1. the code needs to have its items prioritized in a more evident manner. Items need `stacking';

2. the role of the individual as distinct from the role of the organization should be more clearly delineated;

3. there are conflicts among some of the items included in the code;

4. the code represents idealism; then there is realism, the real world;

5. many of the issues raised are not specific to software engineering;

6. would some of the issues be more easily incorporated under a code of business ethics?

7. are some of the issues raised actually about the management of large projects?

8. the code is really a set of guidance notes;

9.is any kind of enforcement to be offered/imposed?

10. some of the names included in the task force appear to be relatively `new' names to the field of ethics.

Going sequentially through the text, the following remarks are offered:

1. Princ. 1: The goals are too high to be achieved! What is then the meaning of such a statement? Some paragraphs have nothing to see with ethics (1.01 `well documented'; 1.05 `ensure appropriate methodology', ...)

2. Princ. 2: In our view, the emphasis must be put on `disclose the potential danger'. About 2.06, one could wonder if it is feasible when we know that software always contains errors! We also insist on the difficulty of locating the responsibilities in software development

3. Princ. 3: Difficult to implement

4. Princ. 4: `consistent with the public health, ...': does it mean that Princ. 4 is ranked over Princ. 3 (see `always act ...')? Where would you include whistle-blowing? In 4.07? Wouldn't it be better to consider it in a general clause?

5. Princ. 5: Curiously most of the statements are related to software engineers in their position towards their subordinates more than to themselves as leaders. It looks like the management (title of Princ. 5) would have the task of enforcing the code! 5.07 (`fair remuneration') may smell as `bribe is allowed in certain limits'. Is it necessary to mention something which is normally regulated by the market?

6. Princ. 7: Being so detailed, why not mention the share of work and responsibility with women. Would it be `gender discrepancy'? But, more precisely, SIG9.2.2 would suggest to insist on specific software engineering aspects and not on too general matters. How to interpret 7.03 when thinking about Microsoft Windows 95 unreliability, or other well known products? 7.07 is also a bit wishful thinking when compared to practice!

7. Princ. 8: There is a real confusion between what must appear as a duty (updating and deepening their knowledge and competence) and what is linked to the code. 8.05 needs explanation (`the law governing the software'???). The title `Self' is meaningless: SIG9.2.2 thinks that `education' would be more appropriate and needs to be stressed as a principle as such, including the present Princ. 8.

Concluding remarks:

1. It would be better to distinguish the statements which belong to `Business Ethics in general' and those which are specific to software engineering as such.

2. As many codes of this kind, the statements are individualistic, whereas software engineering is more and more a collective task, a work of teams where most of the problems are management problems.

3. Such a `code' is too consensual and doesn't recognize the conflicting world in which we evolve. It is needed to recognize the conflicts of interests. For instance, nothing is said about the problem of delocalization of software engineering, whereas it is a real ethical question of justice.

4. Such a `code' would be better named "Guidance notes", and surely "Code of practice" better than "Code of ethics" (many statements have nothing to see with ethics; present title is misguiding).

5. In terms of structure, the three levels as mentioned in the Preamble must appear more clearly in the presentation.

Jacques Berleur asked members of the SIG to send him any further comments on the code. He offered to be in contact with Don Gotterbarn and forward to him our observations.
