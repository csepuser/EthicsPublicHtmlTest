Draft Minutes from Meeting Email from Frailey to Gotterbarn: "Draft Minutes from Meeting" - April 8, 1997 (Level 2) E-mail

Not yet final, but you can see more about the committee's plans with respect to your activites. They were quite pleased with what you are doing but want to be deliberate about things.

DJF

----- Begin Included Message -----

The committee met by conference call on April 8. Members in attendance by conference call were: Felipe Cabrera (briefly) Dennis Frailey Mario Barbacci Elliot Chikofsky Larry Druffel Stu Feldman

Members not in attendance included: Barry Boehm mary Shaw Stu Zweben\ xxxxxxxx (who left some thoughts with Felipe - which were read to the members at the beginning of the meeting)

CODE OF ETHICS AND PROFESSIONAL PRACTICE

The committee agreed that the second draft is more suitable for release for comment than the first, and authorized the task force to release the second draft for comment in newsletters, web pages, and other informal publications. The intent is to solicit commentary from the practioners and other interested parties. The approval is a blanket approval for all publications meeting the criteria below, with the following provisos: - it be clearly marked as a draft for comment - it not be published in any archival journals, as it is not yet complete or final - only to newsletters and other temporary publications that are widely read by practicing software engineers, software engineering educators, and other interested parties. - it be distributed consistently to all publications (same cover memo, identical content, etc. so as to avoid any problems of inconsistency) - a cover memo explaining the background accompany each publication (the cover memo to be approved by the steering committee)

Action: Stu Feldman will work with Don Gotterbarn to develop such a cover memo for committee approval

Action: Mario Barbacci will inform Don of the webmaster of the Computer Society's web pages, who can arrange to have the draft published there

Action: Dennis Frailey will inform Don of the webmaster of the ACM web pages, who can arrange to have the draft published there or to have a link to the Computer Society page (probably the better method to assure consistency)

The committee agreed to issue a certificate of appreciation to those who made major contributions to the two drafts of the code. The certificate will come from both societies and will be distributed at the ICSE conference coming up in May.

 

Action: Dennis Frailey get names of appropriate individuals from Don G. and forward them to Mario

Action: Elliot Chikofsky to arrange for these certificates to be distributed at the awards ceremony at ICSE

The committee agreed that we committee members should each provide feedback to the code of ethics and professional practices task force regarding the second draft of the survey.

Action: All: Provide feedback to Don Gotterbarn on the draft code by no later than May 31.

SURVEY FOR BODY OF KNOWLEDGE

The committee agreed to issue a certificate of appreciation to those who made major contributions to the survey and its analysis. The certificates will come from both societies and will be distributed at the ICSE conference coming up in May.

Action: Mario will obtain names from the analysis report

Action: All will forward to Mario any other names they think should be included

Action: Elliot Chikofsky to arrange for these certificates to be distributed at the awards ceremony at ICSE

After considerable discussion, the committee concluded that a further and more complete dialog among the committee is needed before drawing any conclusions from or making recommendations on the basis of the survey analysis report. The committee agreed to begin an e-mail dialog among committee members to address the issues surrounding the survey: what we should do next, what we learned from it, etc.

Action: Dennis Frailey to distribute a call for the committee dialog and will identify the major topics to be addressed. >> Action: All: respond to the call for dialog with well-conceived analysis and recommendations -- Deadline will be April 30, 1997.

COMMITTEE MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

The committee agreed to establish a regular conference call, perhaps every three weeks, at a time to be agreed upon by the committee members.

Action: Felipe Cabrera to poll members about suitable times and to arrange for the regular conference calls.

The committee decided to defer a decision on the next face to face meeting until we have had a chance to review the email dialog on the survey analysis report.

END

----- End Included Message -----
