code of ethics Email from John Nash to Gotterbarn: "code of ethics- concern over length" - November 24, 1997 (Level 2) E-mail

While I applaud the effort to create the code, I am extremely concerned that the current document is a "kitchen sink" version. In fact, it seems to have the sinks from all the bathrooms too! If we want folk to actually pay attention to a code of ethics, it MUST fit on one page of large type.

Personally, I'd prefer a 1 pager supported with a web-available discussion document providing lots of example situations, well?indexed, providing "situations" where each of the points addressed in the questionnaire are illustrated. People relate to real?world situations that allow for thought and dialogue about the genuine difficulties raised by the pressures of budget and personnel constraints. Such situations are rarely clear?cut; what appears to be an obvious choice of good over evil in a code of ethics becomes a too?quick reaction to a perceived emergency.


Best wishes in a difficult task.

JN

PS I used to be Treasurer for Amnesty International Canada and (because I am bilingual) "a spokesperson", so I am familiar with the difficulties of presenting and codifying ethical and moral problems. Good luck.
