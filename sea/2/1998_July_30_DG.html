CHARTER of the IEEE Computer Society / ACM SOFTWARE ENGINEERING COORDINATING COMMITTEE

As approved on 19 October 1998 by the Council of the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) and xx November 1998 by the Board of Governors of the IEEE Computer Society (CS).

Article I. Name and Purpose Section

1.1 Name

The name of the organization is the Software Engineering Coordinating Committee (hereinafter referred to as "SWECC").

Section 1.2 Purposes The SWECC exists to foster and maintain software engineering as a professional discipline. Toward this end, SWECC will identify, develop, establish and/or maintain various artifacts that support this purpose, such as: identify, validate and maintain the body of knowledge of software engineering, develop and maintain a code of ethics for software engineering practitioners, develop and maintain a code of professional practice, establish and validate performance norms, develop and maintain curriculum guidelines, and recommend assessment criteria. The SWECC will establish and maintain liaison with other national, regional, and international groups that are promulgating software engineering as a discipline so as to maximize harmonization among these activities.

Article II. Structure SWECC shall consist of an Executive Committee and such project committees as it shall establish, as provided in Article IV herein. The SWECC reports to the ACM and the CS.

Article III. Executive Committee

Section 3.1 Purpose The Executive Committee shall be the governing body of SWECC. The Executive Committee shall approve all expenditures from funds assigned to SWECC in accordance with budgets and plans approved by the CS Board of Governors and the ACM Council.

Section 3.2 Number, Selection and Tenure The Executive Committee shall consist of six (6) members. The ACM and the CS shall each appoint three members for such terms as they shall each establish. Vacancies existing by reason of resignation, death, incapacity or removal before the expiration of a member's term shall be filled by the member society which appointed the individual. The chair of the Executive Committee shall serve for two years. The first chair shall be appointed from among the members of the Board by the CS. The second chair shall be similarly appointed by the ACM. The chair will be rotated every two years between appointed representatives from the CS and the ACM. The vice-chair of the Executive Committee shall serve for two years. The first vice-chair shall be appointed from among the members of the Board by the ACM. The second vice-chair shall be similarly appointed by the CS. The vice-chair will be rotated every two years between appointed representatives from the CS and the ACM. The vice-chair shall conduct SWECC meetings in the absence of the chair. The chair and vice-chair shall have responsibility communication among the SWECC, the ACM and the CS. The SWECC may establish and fill other officer positions from its membership as it may deem appropriate.

Section 3.3 Meetings, Attendance, and Voting The Executive Committee shall hold at least one (1) meeting annually and may hold additional meetings as approved by the Executive Committee. Forty-five days notice shall be given for all meetings. Meetings shall be conducted according to Roberts Rules of Order, except when such rules are inconsistent with provisions otherwise provided herein or in policies and procedures developed in accordance with Article IX, below. Voting proxies may be assigned to another member of the Executive Committee. However, at least one voting member from each society must be present for the conduct of business.

Article IV Projects

Section 4.1 Purpose Projects shall produce products or services that fulfill the purposes of SWECC.

Section 4.2 Formation The SWECC Executive Committee may form a project with the consent of the Executive Committee of the ACM and the Executive Committee of the CS. The approval will include the intended duration of the project. Extensions of a project beyond the approved duration will require reauthorization by the CS and the ACM.

Section 4.3 Project Committees Once a project is approved, the Executive Committee will create a project committee for its oversight with equal membership and structure as it shall deem appropriate, provided that the equal partnership of the CS and the ACM shall in each case be maintained.

Section 4.4 Project Advisory Groups A project may with the consent of the Executive Committee form an advisory group to aid in fulfillment of project purposes.

Article V Staff Support

Section 5.1 Staff SWECC shall have no paid staff. The ACM and the CS shall support the activities of SWECC through their respective headquarters operations.

Section 5.2 Secretariat In order to provide an unchanging address to the software engineering community, the CS will provide a secretariat service for SWECC, and its headquarters address, telephone, and FAX number may be listed for SWECC contacts. A general e-mail address will be also be maintained. No charge will be made to SWECC for this service other than documented pass-through charges such as telephone calls and FAX communications. The secretariat services shall be limited to communications pass-through, and shall not include secretarial support of the committee or its chair. The explicit intent of the societies in establishing the SWECC is that it be a volunteer activity. However, each society may provide support to its members serving on the SWECC, including the chair, to the extent that it may deem appropriate to do so.

Article VI Finances

Section 6.1 Fiscal Year The fiscal year for SWECC shall be July 1 to June 30.

Section 6.2 Budget Prior to the start of each fiscal year, the Executive Committee shall develop and approve a budget. Expenses of the Executive Committee and all projects shall be kept in accordance with the approved budget.

Section 6.3 Expenses All expenses of members of the Executive Committee incurred in the conduct of the work, including the expenses of members who serve as SWECC officer, will be paid by the individual members or their sponsoring society.

Section 6.4 Assessment to ACM and CS The ACM and the CS shall support the expenses of SWECC by paying annual assessments equal to the net operating cost of SWECC in excess of those expenses covered by donations from corporate sponsors. The amount of annual assessments will be recommended by the Executive Committee and subject to approval of the CS and the ACM through their budgetary mechanisms.

Section 6.5 Expense Sharing The ACM and the CS will establish a common framework for measuring costs and contributions from each society. Only those costs directly related to meetings and activities initiated by the Executive Committee shall be considered general operating costs of SWECC to be shared by and paid through assessments to the ACM and the CS. All costs associated with meetings and other activities initiated by a project shall be considered operating costs of a project, to be paid by assessment shared by the CS and the ACM and donations from corporate sponsors.

Section 6.6 Deposits and Accounts Normally, SWECC shall not accumulate cash or other assets, and it is anticipated that most operating costs will be covered by the CS who, which will then seek reimbursement for half of the cost from the ACM. A separate operating account shall not be necessary, but the CS will maintain an accounting fund for purposes of segregating all monies received by the SWECC in form of donations or other sources.

Section 6.7 Assets Should SWECC acquire any assets, all such assets shall be jointly owned by the ACM and the CS, including goodwill, copyright and other intellectual property such as trademarks and trade secrets of SWECC and any inventories or other properties, or cash as may from time to time be obtained. In the event of dissolution of SWECC, any remaining assets shall be distributed equally to the ACM and the CS in a manner approved by both the Executive Committee of the ACM and the Executive Committee of the CS.

Section 6.8 Fiscal Agent The CS will serve as the fiscal agent for the SWECC.

Article VII Reports

Section 7.1 Annual Report The chair of the SWECC shall submit an annual report describing its programs, activities and accomplishments to the Council of the ACM and the Board of Governors of the CS. This report, describing the activities of the prior calendar year, shall be due on March 1 of each year.

Section 7.2 Financial Report Financial reports shall be provided at least annually to the executive directors of the ACM and the CS by the chair of the SWECC. This report, describing the prior fiscal year, shall be due on September 1 of each year.

Article VIII Amendments Amendments to this charter may be proposed by a majority vote of all members of the SWECC Executive Committee and will become effective immediately upon the approval by both the Executive Committee of the ACM and the Executive Committee of the CS.

Article IX Policies and Procedures This Charter may be amplified by Policies and Procedures, developed and approved by the SWECC Executive Committee, with the consent of the Executive Committee of the ACM and the Executive Committee of the CS.
