Process and Examination Process and Examination for Software Engineers - suggestions - 1995? (Level 2) Electronic document

PROCESS & EXAMINATION

3 basic requirement(s) -- "a process model"

1. Education

2. Experience (extra experience generally allowed to substitute for some degree of education)

3. Examination: (2 parts)

o "Fundamentals of Engineering" (FE exam)

-- aimed at assessing what the engineer has learned during their educational experience. (Some Boards will accept extra education (e.g, Ph.D.) in leu of this exam. This exam is taken first.

- Passed exam is known as "Engineering Intern." (Don Gotterbarn asks is this equivalent to an "Engineer In Training ÕEITþ?")

- Internship lasts a minimum of 4 years, then take 2nd exam.

- Starting April-96 exam sp" to two modules: . Lower Division (common knowledge) . Upper Division (discipline specific), based on modules like Civil, Mechanical, Chemical, Electrical, Industrial, more modules possible (Computer Engineering contemplated

-- note that this is a "hardware/chips" person). o "Principles and Practices of Engineering (P&PE)" exam

-- aimed at what a practitioner would learn during the first 4 years about impact on the safety, health and welfare of the public.

- See below for current process/model

- Future proposals (follow on from FE deltas)

- Ethical Practices module - Technical Specialty module

Revised process models are being considered as a complement to the FE and P&PE exam deltas discussed:

A. Grant PE after FE exam completion plus period of internship (time length based on "perceived" quality of education.

B. Grant PE as in "A." but include Ethics Exam.

C. Grant PE as in "B." but with internship in recognized specialty area and subsequent examination in that specialty.

CURRENT EXAMS:

o Based on extensive surveys of practicing professionals about knowledge level needed:

- "NCEES Analysis of Professional Activities and Requirements of the Engineering Profession" (master document discussing survey process and appendices covering each discipline, such as mechanical, electrical, etc.)

- Continual survey update process is planned (first planned for Civil) . survey update to be done as part of each PP&E exam application process

o Current PP&E exams based on choosing 4 questions (out of 12) for each of the morning and afternoon parts. Questions are intended to be of equivalent difficulty and should require 1 hour per question. Authors of questions recognize difficulty in achieving consistency of difficulty and time requirements.

- Proposal to select between fixed sets of 8-hour examinations; sometimes there will be a choice amongst 4-hour exams (in afternoon) that follow a common 4-hour exam (in morning). This would allow for the further specialization of the P&PE exam.

- Adoption proposed for Fall 95, w/implementation no earlier then Spring 97.

- PE's willing to write questions for this module (stipend provided @NCEES expense).
