I sent this message to Dennis last night and he suggested I share it with the whole steering committee. MRB

About ten days ago I got a message from Barry about an inquiry from Dave Parnas. I gather he is unhappy because we are not addressing licensing/certification issues. I am not sure how strongly he feels about this but just in case, I am enclosing all the correspondence so you are all aware of Dave's concern.

I called the NSPE this morning and they will send me material describing their products and services. I have also asked for material from the National Council of Examiners for Engineering and Surveying (NCEE).

Mario

Barry,

No, we have not had contact with the NSPE but we are aware of them. The problem is the classical chicken and egg. It would be better if we had something concrete e.g., a draft of the survey instrument so we can meet with them and get their input.

If I recall it correctly, these are the folks that advise legislatures and others about certification procedures and such. Since the steering committee has not chartered a task force to look at licensing/certification (too early in my opinion) it would seem premature but maybe I am wrong.

Re. the APEO (Ontario), we have gotten expressions of interest from other national societies and we will work with them. I am meeting with the IEE and the BCS in a couple of weeks and that will be the start of the international cooperation.

Please give Dave my regards and reassure him that this is not a matter of excluding or competing with other bodies. It is just that we move slowly.

Regards,

Mario

Hi Barry,

I am happy to hear that the ethics group has done the right thing, but rather disappointed with Mario's reply. It's better to know what the situation is, before drafting an action plan. Engineering Societies have been thinking about all of these issues for a long long time. Picking a position first, and then finding out what they have done and what their position is and what they have to offer is indeed a way to move slowly. The trouble is that one moves slowly but surely into unnecessary conflict.

Dave
