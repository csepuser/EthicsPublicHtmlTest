CODE OF ETHICS FOR SOFTWARE ENGINEERS v1.0 September 1996

INTRODUCTION

Computers now have a central and growing role in commerce, industry, government, medicine, entertainment, and ordinary life. Because the utility of computers depends in large part on the instructions written for them, those who design, develop, and test software have enormous opportunities both to do good and to cause harm.

To assure, as much as possible, that this power will be used for good, software engineers commit themselves to making the design, development, and testing of software a distinct, beneficial, and respected profession. In accordance with that commitment, software engineers shall adhere to the following standards of conduct.

The seven main paragraphs state general rules. Each subsidiary clause is a specific application of its general rule, one experience has shown needs express statement; but no set of subsidiary clauses exhausts the general rule.

Rule 1: PRODUCT.

Software engineers shall, insofar as possible, assure that the software on which they work is useful to public, employer, customer, and user, completed on time and at reasonable cost, and free of significant error. In particular, software engineers shall, as appropriate:

1.01. Assure that specifications for software on which they work have been put in writing, satisfy the user's requirements, and have the customer's approval. PMI IIb - ACM 3.4

1.02. Assure that they understand fully the specifications for software on which they work. PMI IIb - ACM 3.4

1.03. Assure that they are qualified, by education and experience, for any project on which they work. PE II2a - IEEE 6 - PMI Ib - ICCP 2.5

1.04. Assure proper goals and objectives for any project on which they work. PMI IIb - ACM 3.4

1.05. Assure proper development methodology on any project on which they work. PMI IIb - ACM 3.4

1.06. Assure proper management on any project on which they work, including proper procedures for control of quality and risk. PMI IIb - ACM 2.1

1.07. Assure proper estimates of cost, schedule, personnel, and outcome on any project on which they work. PMI IIb

1.08. Assure adequate documentation on any project on which they work, including a log of problems discovered and solutions adopted. PMI IIb - ACM 3.4

1.09. Assure proper testing, debugging, and review of software and related documents on which they work. PMI IIb - ACM 3.4

1.10. Assure that software and related documents on which they work respect the privacy of those who will be subject to the software. PMI IIb - ACM 1.7

1.11 Assure that raw information used in software is accurate, derives from a legitimate source, and is used only in ways properly authorized. ACM 1.7,1.8 - ICCP 2.8

1.12. Assure ethical, economic, cultural, legal, and environmental issues are properly identified, defined, and addressed. ACM 2.3 - ICCP 2.7

1.13. Promote maximum productivity and minimum cost to employer, customer, user, and public. PMI IIa - ACM 2.1 - ICCP 2.7

1.14. Avoid fads, departing from standard practices only when justified. ACM 2.1

Rule 2: PUBLIC.

Software engineers shall, in their professional role, act only in ways consistent with the public safety, health and welfare. In particular, software engineers shall:

2.01. Disclose to appropriate persons any danger that the software or related documents on which they work may pose to the user, a third party, or the environment. PE II1a - PMI IVa - ACM 3.1 - ICCP 3.7

2.02. Approve software only if they have a well-documented belief that it is safe, meets specifications, and has passed all appropriate tests. PE II1a - PMI IVa - ACM 3.2

2.03. Affix their signature only to documents prepared under their supervision and within their areas of competence. PE II1b - PMI Ib - ACM 3.2 - ICCP 2.7

2.04. Cooperate in efforts to correct problems in software or related documents. PE I4 - ACM 1.3

2.05. Be fair and truthful in all public statements concerning software or related documents. PE II3 - ICCP 3.4

2.06. Not put self-interest, the interest of an employer, or the interest of a client or customer ahead of the public's interest. PE III1f - ACM 1.1

2.07. Accept full responsibility for their own work. PE III1a - PE III9 - IEEE 1 - PMI Ia - ACM 2.4

Rule 3: JUDGMENT.

Software engineers shall, insofar as possible, protect both the independence of their professional judgment and their reputation for such judgment. In particular, software engineers shall, as appropriate:

3.01. Maintain professional skepticism with respect to any software or related documents they are asked to evaluate. ACM 2.5

3.02. Reject bribery. IEEE 4

3.03. Accept no payback, kickback, or other payment from a third party to a contract, except with the knowledge and consent of all parties to the contract. PE II4c - PE II5a - PMI IIId

3.04. Accept payment from only one party for any particular project, or for services related to the same project, except when the circumstances have been fully disclosed to the parties concerned and they have given their informed consent. PE II4b - ACM 2.6

3.05. Neither solicit nor accept a contract from a governmental body on which a principal or officer of their employer serves as a member. PE II 4e

3.06. Participate in no decision of a governmental or professional body, as a member or advisor, concerned with software, or related documents, in which they, their employer, or their client have a financial interest. PE II4d

3.07. Disclose to all concerned parties those conflicts of interest that cannot reasonably be avoided or escaped. PE II4a - IEEE 2 - PMI IIIc

Rule 4: CLIENT AND EMPLOYER.

Software engineers shall, consistent with the public health, safety, and welfare, always act in professional matters as faithful agents and trustees of their client or employer. In particular, software engineers shall:

4.01. Provide service only in areas of competence. PE II2 - IEEE 6 - PMI Ib - ICCP 2.5

4.02. Assure that any document upon which they rely has been approved by someone qualified to approve it. PE II2c - PMI IIIa

4.03. Use the property of a client or employer only in ways properly authorized. PMI IIIa - ACM 3.3,2.8

4.04. Not knowingly use pirated software on equipment of a client or employer or in work performed for a client or employer. ACM 3.3,1.5

4.05. Keep as confidential information gained in their professional work that is not properly in the public domain. PE II1c - PMI IIIa - ACM 1.8 - ICCP 2.1

4.06. Identify, document, properly report to employer or client any problem in the software or related documents on which they work. ACM 2.5

4.07. Inform client or employer promptly if, in their opinion, a project is likely to fail, to prove too expensive, to violate copyright laws, or otherwise to turn out badly. PE III1b - PMI IIIa - ACM 2.5

4.08. Accept no outside work detrimental to the work they perform for their primary employer. PE III1c - PMI IIIa - ACM 2.5 - ICCP 3.6

4.09. Represent no interest adverse to their employer's without the employer's consent. PE III4b - PMI IIIa - 2.6 - ICCP 3.6

Rule 5: PROFESSION.

Software engineers shall, in all professional matters, advance both the integrity and reputation of their profession. In particular, software engineers shall, insofar as possible:

5.01. Associate only with reputable businesses. PE III9b

5.02. Assure that clients, employers, and supervisors know of this code of ethics. IEEE 10 - PMI Ie - ACM 4.1,4.2

5.03. Support software engineers who do as this code requires. IEEE 10 - PMI Ie - ACM 4.1

5.04. Help develop an organizational environment favorable to acting ethically. PMI Ie

5.05. Report violations of this code to appropriate authorities. PE II1e - ICCP 3.8

5.06. Take responsibility for detecting, correcting, and reporting errors in software and associated documents on which they work. PMI Ia

5.07. Only accept a salary appropriate to professional qualifications. PE III6

5.08. Be accurate in stating the characteristics of software on which they work, avoiding not only false claims but claims that might reasonably be supposed to be deceptive, misleading, or doubtful. PE II3 - IEEE 3 - ACM 2.5 - ICCP 3.4

5.09. Not promote their own interest at the expense of the profession. PE III1f

5.10. Obey all laws governing their work, insofar as such obedience is consistent with the public health, safety, and welfare. PMI Ig - ICCP 3.2

5.11. Serve in civic affairs constructively. PE III2a

5.12. Improve public knowledge of software engineering. IEEE 5 - PMI IVb - ACM 2.7 - ICCP 2.2

5.13. Share useful software-related knowledge, inventions, or discoveries with the profession by reading papers at professional meetings, by publishing articles in the technical press, and by serving on the profession's standard-setting bodies. PE III11b,3c - ICCP 3.1

5.14. Make no political contribution, gift, or commission for award of a contract. PE II5b

Rule 6: COLLEAGUES.

Software engineers shall treat all those with whom they work fairly. In particular, software engineers shall, as appropriate:

6.01. Assist co-workers in professional development. PE III11 - IEEE 10 - PMI IIg - ACM 3.6

6.02. Review the work of other professionals only with their knowledge. PE III8a - IEEE 7 - PMI II

6.03. Credit fully the work of others. PE III10a - IEEE 7 - PMI IIf - ACM 1.6 - ICCP 1.8

6.04. Criticize the work of others in an objective, candid, and properly-documented way. PE III8 - IEEE 7 - PMI IIf - ACM 2.4 - ICCP 2.6

6.05. Give a fair hearing to the opinion, concern, or complaint of a colleague. PMI IIc

6.06. Assure that employees are informed of standards before being held to them. PE III11e - PMI IIc - ICCP 2.7

6.07. Assure co-workers know the employer's policies and procedures for protecting passwords, files, and other confidential information. PE III11e - PMI IIc - ICCP 2.7

6.08. Assign work only upon considerations of professional qualifications. PE III11d

6.09. Provide for due process in hearing charges of violation of an employer's policy or of this code. PMI IIc

6.10. Develop a fair agreement concerning ownership of any invention an employee makes. PE III10c

6.11. Not supplant another software engineer after steps have been taken for employment. PE III 11e

6.12. Attract employees only by full and accurate description of the conditions of employment. PE III11e

6.13. Offer only fair and just compensation. PE III6b

6.14. Not prevent a subordinate from taking a better job for which the subordinate is qualified. IEEE 8

Rule 7: SELF.

Software engineers shall, throughout their career, try to enhance their own ability to practice their profession as it should be practiced. In particular, software engineers shall continually endeavor to:

7.01. Improve their knowledge of recent developments in the design, development, and testing of software and related documents. PE III11a - PMI Ic - ACM 2.2 - ICCP 3.1

7.02. Improve their ability to create safe, reliable, and useful software at reasonable cost and within a reasonable time. PE III11a PMI Ic - ACM 2.2 - ICCP 3.1

7.03. Improve their ability to write accurate, informative, and literate documents in support of software on which they work. PE III11a PMI Ic - ACM 2.2 - ICCP 3.1

7.04. Improve their understanding of the software and related documents on which they work and of the environment in which they will be used. PE III11a - ACM 2.2 - ICCP 3.1

7.05. Improve their knowledge of the law governing the software and related documents on which they work. PE III11a - PMI Ig - ACM 2.2 - ICCP 3.1

7.06. Improve their knowledge of this code, its interpretation, and its application to their work. IEEE 10 - PMI Ie - ACM 4.1
