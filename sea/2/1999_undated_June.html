Report of IEEE CS and ACM
SOFTWARE ENGINEERING COORDINATING COMMITTEE

Leonard L. Tripp
Chair

This report provides information on committee activities since last report, June 1999. The report is organized into two parts: (1) committee operations and (2) project progress. The committee has four active projects: code of ethics and professional practice, education guide to software engineering body of knowledge and survey of graduate programs:

I . COMMITTEE OPERATIONS

The committee met in Montreal, Quebec on July 12, 1999 with the University of Quebec at Montreal as the host. The purpose of the meeting was to organize the committee, endorse projects and establish relationships between projects. Attendees were Mark Ardis, Doris Carver, Dennis Frailey, and Leonard Tripe. The committee gassed the following resolutions:
Resolution 3 -Priority for Education Project .The undergraduate curriculum model should contain the care content of the software engineering field.


The review draft of each chapter of the guide has been prepared. Effort is underway to recruit the broadbased set of reviewers. The list of reviewers is available on the web site. Invitations to more than 1 computing societies, engineering societies, and licensing authorities have been sent.

PROJECT STATUS
Code of Ethics and Professional Practice
The project prepared its 1999 plan (see attachment A). Activities are underway to promote the use of the Cede of Ethics and Professional Practice.

Plans for next period November 1999 to March 2000 Work with the Education project to develop Cede of Ethics educational materials.

Education Project The project prepared its 1999 plan (see attachment B). During March 1999 the Education project worked with the SWEBOK project, the Curriculum 2001 project and the Working Group on Software Engineering Education and Training to put in place a method for promoting cooperation among the parties interested in software engineering education.

Plans for next period November 1999 to March 2000 -located meeting with SWEBOK project in Montreal

Guide to Software Engineering Body of Knowledge Project
Meeting of the Industrial Advisory Board were held on July 13 and 14 1999 in Montreal, Quebec. The following items were approved: baseline list of related disciplines, remaining knowledge area specialists, and knowledge area description specification.
