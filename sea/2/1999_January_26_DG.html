Standards of Business Ethics for PSG Professionals/Engineering Code of Conduct

DRAFT 1/26/99

You are Part of the Ethics Team
Dell Computer Corporation

Your Responsibility as a Dell Employee

As a PSG professional you have influence over product development activities at Dell and must be aware of the temptations presented to you and rise above them. Your business behavior must be above reproach and suspicion to ensure Dell’s continued success as a company known for the highest integrity.

We have a serious problem if your actions are perceived as unethical.

If you know or suspect the occurrence of any unethical actions, report them to the appropriate Dell personnel. Start with you manager unless he or she in involved in the suspected activity, in which case you should contact Dell’s legal department or Compliance Office (1-888-888-9975).

Stand Tall and Resist Temptation

Dell has built a solid reputation as a company that maintains the highest of ethical standards. You must distinguish between behavior that is acceptable and that which is not. When appropriate, say “no” and ask questions until the correct actions are clear. You are an important part of the Dell team and are responsible for maintaining Dell’s good reputation.

This booklet is limited in scope and does not cover all of Dell’s Code of Conduct. Rather it focuses on the primary principles that apply to the PSG Professional. Of course, you are responsible for abiding by the entire Code of Conduct and any other Dell policies and practices.

Don’t be Trapped!

You are a target for temptation when you commit Dell funds or influence the commitment of Dell funds. Dell’s business partners will attempt to influence your decisions and cause you to show preference for their products and services.

Resist such temptations when a business partner tries to ply you with gifts and favors. Base business decisions on product quality, competitive pricing, delivery, and provider reputation. Dell understands that in the course of business, you will establish relationships with business partners. Nonetheless, you must maintain a businesslike relationship with the highest degree of integrity.

Your practices will determine the success of Dell’s relationships with its business partners and customers. This booklet highlights the standards of conduct Dell expects of you.


How Shall I Act as a PSG Professional?

PSG engineering and program management staff should strive to attain the highest professional standard of performance.

Engineering and program management staff shall hold paramount the safety, health, and welfare of the public in the performance of their professional duties. Any issue regarding product safety and regulatory compliance must be reprinted to the Dell Regulatory Group and be ___?____ through the product safety organization.

All PSG Professionals should continue to expand this professional development and must provide opportunities for career development for their staff engineers under their supervisor.

Engineering and program management staff should undertake the product development tasks for others only if qualified by training and experience, or after full disclosure of pertinent limitations.

Engineers and program management staff shall seek out, accept and offer honest criticism of technical analysis and work, and shall forthrightly acknowledge and correct error and credit properly the contributions of others.

Canon of Ethics for Product Planning and the PrP Process

Product engineering and program management staff shall ensure that their products and related modifications are of the highest professional standard possible. In particular, product personnel shall:

a) use best effort for high quality, acceptable cost and a reasonable schedule, ensuring that trade-offs are communicated to upper management;
b) strive to identify, define, and address ethical, cultural, legal, and environmental issues related to work projects;
c) work to follow industry standard when available that are best appropriate for the task at hand, departing from these only when technically justified;
d) strive to fully understand the specifications for the program;
e) ensure that the specifications are well documented and satisfy the business contract and have appropriate approvals;
f) ensure realistic estimates of cost, scheduling, personnel and __?___ of any project and provide a risk assessment of these estimates;
g) ensure adequate testing and review of the product;
h) use only accurate data derived by ethical and lawful means and used only in ways properly authorized;
i) maintain the integrity of data.

Acceptable and Unacceptable Behavior

Your relationship with business partners must be arms-length and well documented. While negotiation with business partners, base your decisions on measurable and sound business judgment.

Undue favoritism and preferential treatment are unacceptable behaviors, which compromise Dell’s reputation.

Accepting favors is not permitted. This is a two-way street; you may not give favors to business partners and you may not accept favors from them.

Accepting gifts of an influencing nature, complimentary services and non-public discounts on personal supplies or services violate Dell policy and practice. This prohibition includes trips and other entertainment not reciprocal in nature and not directly related to Dell business.

If you receive a gift with a value greater than $50, you are to return it to the vendor and explain Dell’s policy regarding gifts. Be pleasant and professional, but firm.

Investments in business partners (Conflicts of Interest)

Keep separate any personal and business activities that my give rise to a conflict of interest. Specifically, you may not have a financial interest, hold a position or have a relationship with any person, firm or company that conducts business with Dell if that could influence your decisions as a PSG professional.

If you hold shares in a publicly traded company which supplies products or services to Dell, that interest cannot exceed 1% of the value of the outstanding stock or 1% of the series or class of securities. If your interest exceeds those amounts, you may not participate in any business decision regarding that supplier. Also you must advise your manager or vice president that you have a potential conflict of interest. If Dell deems your interest significant enough to warrant a change, you may be reassigned to different duties.

If the company in question is privately held, you must report any interest or position held in that company to your manager or vice president. Again, if Dell deems your interest significant enough to warrant a change of assignment, you may find that your duties change as a result of this interest. This includes warrants and stock options for companies which have not yet gone public.

This rule applies to your spouse, children, significant other or any other relationship which may influence your decision. When unsure, discuss the situation with your manager or the Dell Legal Department.

Document Your Relationship

One way to avoid disputes with business partners is to meticulously document meetings and phone conversations. This also serves to provide a record for other Dell employees who may need to understand our history with a business partner. Therefore, after meetings and phone conversations, take the time to make appropriate notes of the conversation with special emphasis on action items resulting from the discussion. Dell’s reputation can depend on our meeting any commitments which arise out of our continued relationships with our partners.

Also, be very careful about your use of electronic mail with business partners. It is easy to become too informal in your communications using this method. An electronic mail may, in some instances, be used as evidence against Dell in a dispute. Accordingly, read your mail carefully before sending it to a business partner to ensure that it does not commit Dell to something unintended by you, or which conflicts with Dell’s underlying agreement with the partner.

Especially important is to recognize that when you make verbal commitments or requests to partners, those partners may act and commit resources which could involve financial exposure to Dell. Therefore, you should be sure to explain to the partner that you will be following the conversation with a written memorandum or letter agreement which sets out the arrangement prior to Dell being bound by any verbal commitment. Then be sure to contact Dell’s Legal Department in a timely manner to work out the necessary agreements or other memoranda of your intended arrangement.

And remember, all contracts with business partners require legal review and approval.

Your Relationship with Partners

Your relationship with partners should be cordial but definitely businesslike. All communications should be coordinated with WW Procurement. Be objective and exercise sound judgment in decision making. Base your relationship on mutual respect and integrity. PSG employees are not authorized to make procurement decisions!

You may give a business partner Dell promotional items in the course of normal business activities. Token gifts should be of nominal value and given to help maintain a friendly and professional relationship.

So, what may you accept? Business partner gifts of pens, huggies, cups, notepads, key chains, etc. may be accepted. These gifts should not be of value to influence you.

Again, an acceptable gift may not exceed a market value of $50. If you are unsure of the gift’s value, return the gift promptly and politely to the vendor and explain Dell’s policy.

Dell discourages PSG professionals from routinely attending meals with suppliers or business partners unless the meal visits Dell’s site, Dell should pick up the tab for the meal. If you visit a partner it is acceptable to allow the supplier to host the lunch or dinner.

PSG professionals should not regularly participate in non-business related activities such as golf outing, sports events, and concerts. Remember, maintain an arms-length relationship with Dell’s partners and avoid the temptations to be unduly influenced.

If you are invited to attend entertainment which is not part of a legitimate business meeting, you must obtain the prior approval of a Director or Vice President in your department. Vice Presidents and Directors must use sound judgment in deciding whether to attend or to approve participation in such an event.

Confidential Information

Know what “confidential information” is and do not disclose it to those who should hot have access. This rule applies to both Dell and partner information that is confidential.

On occasion, partners will tell you proprietary information about their company. You must respect the confidential nature of such information and keep it to yourself. Discussing confidential information with inappropriate people is a violation of Dell policy and could be a violation of law.

When you suspect that information provided to you may be confidential, encourage the supplier to mark the information appropriately.

Also, be vigilant regarding your disclosure of Dell’s confidential information to suppliers. Pricing, volumes, product road maps, specifications and drawings are only a few examples of Dell confidential information. Disclose confidential information only when there is a valid and compelling business reason to do so, and only to those suppliers with whom Dell has a valid and effective Nondisclosure Agreement.

Standards Setting Activities

Engineers and Program Managers who are engaged in standard setting activities must take special care in avoiding legal problems involving antitrust concerns and other anti competitive issues.

Specifically, engineers and program managers must avoid any standard setting activities which are a detriment to industry participants.

In addition, Dell has adopted strict guidelines regarding standard setting activities which are available on the legal home page at inside.dell.com.

Software Privacy

Dell strives to enforce and abide by all of its contractual commitments to its suppliers and vendors. Dell recognizes that software belonging to our suppliers is their intellectual property. Accordingly, it is Dell’s policy that all employees not knowingly use software that is obtained or retained either illegally or unethically. Engineers and program managers bear a special responsibility for ensuring that policy is adhered to.

Engineers and Program Management staff who become involved in standard setting activities shall refrain from invitations or becoming involved in decisions regarding:

· the establishment of industry-wide prices terms and conditions of sale or marketing policies;
· allocations of customers, markets, or productions quotas;
· proprietary designs or productions methods without appropriate notice or ___?_____
of rights; or impositions of sanctions on competitor suppliers or customers. SES members who anticipate in standardization activities where such subjects are mentioned should take immediate action to terminate any discussion concerning these subjects. If such discussion is not terminated the SES members should announce their concern and immediately leave the meeting. SES members should have the proper regard for the safety, health and welfare of the public in the performance of their professional duties. If an engineering judgment is overruled by a non-technical authority, the member should clearly point out the consequences. An SES member should notify the proper authority of any observed condition which provides the potential for endangering public safety or health.

Moonlighting

Employees of PSG any not moonlight without first receiving written permission from their supervisor. Moonlighting may cause the inadvertent release of confidential information and the distraction of the employee from his primary duties.

Traveling Abroad

When you travel, you will find that business ethics of persons in other countries may differ from those in your country. Dell expects its employees to adhere to the spirit of the standards in this book wherever they may go. Nevertheless, there may be instances where you will find that exceptions must be made in order to avoid insult or spoiling a valued relationship.

For example:

· When you are in a foreign country, you will often find that parties wish to host a dinner in your honor. It is impractical and often insulting to your host if you refuse to attend. Therefore, it is permissible to attend a dinner; however, try to avoid more than one such dinner for each visit.
· Gifts are often given when you visit partners overseas. If you receive a gift that is promotional in nature (i.e. has the supplier’s name or logo), you are not obligated to return the gift (you are never allowed to solicit such a gift, however). If you are given a gift which is not promotional in nature and clearly exceeds the $50 limit described above, or if the gift consists of the supplier’s product (i.e. software, a modem, etc.), use your judgment. If the gift is so valuable that it is clearly a bribe or attempt to sway your opinion, return the gift immediately and report the event to your manager. Otherwise, for gifts which are clearly given as a matter of course and, in your reasonable opinion, returning the gift would insult your host, accept it graciously. Then, when you return, give the gift to your manager with a report of the circumstances. The gift will be regarded as Dell property and disposed of appropriately.
· In some countries your host will suggest entertainment that would not be appropriate in your country or which would violate Dell’s policy against sexual discrimination or harassment. This includes nightclubs with adult entertainment, and entertainment where either women or men are not included because of their gender. If you are personally uncomfortable with your host’s suggestions for entertainment or if the entertainment would be inappropriate in its sexual or other content such that it would violate Dell’s policy against sexual discrimination and/or harassment, explain Dell’s policy to your host and request an alternative.
· In some countries people will indulge in evening social activities which involve a significant amount of alcohol and very late nights. Remember that you are always a representative of Dell, even when you are traveling, and your demeanor, even during social events, is closely watched by representatives of your suppliers. The excessive use of alcohol can result not only in embarrassing you and Dell, but can also lead to an inadvertent disclosure of information which should not be in the hands of our suppliers.

Etiquette

As a representative of Dell, you are expected to use appropriate etiquette when dealing with partners. This includes avoiding the pitfall of denigrating the products of others. Although you may, from time to time, find yourself confronted with a partner who disparages a competitor’s product (either your competitor or the partner’s competitor) you should avoid taking part in such a conversation.

Objective comparisons are permissible, if they are based on factual and readily proven data. Subjective comments and insults are unnecessary and unproductive. In extreme circumstances such comments could create legal liability for Dell and for you!

Law and Regulations

Dell is committed to the strict observance of laws and regulations of the townships, states and countries in which we do business.
