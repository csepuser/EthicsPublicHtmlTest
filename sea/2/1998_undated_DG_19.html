Recirculated ballot for another standard Email from Gotterbarn to Tripp: "Recirculated ballot for another standard -significant changes made" - September, 1998 (Level 2) E-mail

Leonard,

I just received a recirculated ballot for another standard and noticed that there was a list of the significant changes that were made to the document to make balloter's reappraisal of the modified document easier. I did not send you such a list, so here is my version of the "significant" changes we made.

Strengthened references to maintenance: Principle 5- Management: now explicitly references the management of maintenance 1.05 explicitly lists responsibility to the public for maintenance and support 3.05 insert the phrase "all forms of" before "software maintenance" to make clear that this principle refers to corrective, adaptive and enhancing maintenance

Strengthened Principle 8, which had merely referred to the Software Engineer's commitment to life long learning, to include a commitment to professional practice by adding " and promote an ethical approach to the practice of the profession."

Added "privacy" to the list of concerns in 1.03. Also broadened 1.03 so it would not rule out work for defense industries.

Modified Clause 1.08 to encourage rather than require voluntary contribution of professional skills by inserting "Be encouraged to" before "volunteer"

In 3.09 and 5.05 "risk assessment" is changed to "uncertainty assessment" to more accurately convey that cost, schedule, and personnel projections are estimates.

In consistency with the emphasis on life long learning of Principle 8, inserted "relevant standards and" between "knowledge of" and "the laws governing..."

Added a non-discrimination clause 8.07 and moved the original 8.07 and 8.08 to 8.08 and 8.09 respectively.

Numerous grammatical changes where also made to reduce ambiguity and to correct grammar, e.g, "judgement" to "judgment", "consistent" to "consistently" , "methodology" to "method" , "literate" to "well-written", etc.

I hope this helps.

Don
