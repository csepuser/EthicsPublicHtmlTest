IEEE-CS/ACM Software Engineering Coordinating Committee (SWECC)
Draft Plan
Leonard L. Tripp, Chair, Dennis Frailey, Co-Chair

1. Charter

The Software Engineering Coordinating Committee (SWECC) of the IEEE Computer Society and the ACM exists to foster and maintain software engineering as a professional discipline. Toward this end, SWECC will identify, develop, establish and/or maintain various artifacts that support this purpose, such as: identify and validate the body of knowledge, develop and maintain a code of ethics, develop and maintain code of professional practice, establish and validate performance norms, develop and maintain a model curriculum, and recommend assessment criteria for the profession of software engineering.

2. Committee Organization

Tasks

· Prepare charter for Software Engineering Coordinating Committee
· Prepare ACM implementation instructions
· Prepare CS implementation instructions
· Appoint Coordinating Committee members
· Appoint chair of Coordinating Committee
· Appoint vice-chair of Coordinating Committee
· Develop SWECC Master Plan
· Develop SWECC annual budget
· Develop plan for transitioning existing projects to SWECC jurisdiction

Milestones

M1 - Agreement Definition and Negotiation: Target date - November 1998
Completion criteria: Agreement approved by ACM and IEEE CS

5. Code of Ethics Project

Purpose

To document the ethical and professional responsibilities and obligations of software engineers.

Tasks

· Form balloting group
· Conduct ballot
· Resolve ballot comments
· Prepare comment disposition report
· Recirculate revised code of ethics and comment disposition report
· Submit final code of ethics and comment disposition report
· Prepare plan for fostering the code of ethics

Milestones

M2 - Code of Ethics Approval: Target date - November 1998
Completion criteria: Ballot results approved by ACM and IEEE CS


6. Body of Knowledge Guide Project (Stoneman version)

Purpose

To characterize the contents of the software engineering discipline, provide a topical access structure to the software engineering body of knowledge, clarify boundaries with related disciplines, provide a foundation for curiculum development and individual certification and licensing material, and promote a consistent view of software engineering worldwide.

Tasks

· Establish industry advisory board
· Define list of knowledge areas for software engineering
· Identify knowledge areas from related disciplines that interface with software engineering
· Define topics for each Knowledge Area
· Identify reference material for each Knowledge Area
· Validate guide to software engineering body of knowledge
· Develop approach for moving from Stoneman SWEBOK Guide to Ironman SWEBOK Guide

Milestones

M3 – Initial (Strawman) set of Knowledge Areas for Software Engineering: Target date - September 1998
Completion criteria: Results accepted by Joint Steering Committee Chair and Co-chair

M4 - Body of Knowledge Plan: Target date - November 1998
Completion criteria: Approved by Software Engineering Coordinating Committee

M6 - Baseline Knowledge Areas for Software Engineering :Target date - March 1999
Completion criteria: Approved by Software Engineering Coordinating Committee
M7 - Guide to Software Engineering Body of Knowledge (Stoneman): Target date - August 1999
Completion criteria: Validation results approved by ACM and IEEE CS


7. Performance Norms for Software Engineering Tasks

Purpose

To identify the knowledge/skill needed at different points in the career of a Software Engineer, in particular a graduate no experience and a graduate with four years experience. The software engineering tasks will be described in a document known as a test specification.

Tasks

· Develop survey methodology
· Characterize the engineer plus 4 years experience
· Prepare performance norm questionnaire
· Identify survey population demographics
· Develop approach for obtaining survey participants
· Obtain commitments from survey participants
· Conduct survey
· Ensure survey coverage
· Analyze survey responses
· Prepare performance norms
· Validate performance norms

Milestones

M5 - Performance Norm Plan: Target date – January 1999
Completion criteria: Approved by Software Engineering Coordinating Committee

M8 - Software Engineering Performance Norms: Target date - December 1999
Completion criteria: Ballot results approved by ACM and IEEE CS


8. Education Project

Purpose

To develop software engineering education recommendations in concert with the Guide to the Software Engineering Body of Knowledge.

Tasks

· Complete proposed SW engineering accreditation criteria for undergraduate software engineering programs
· Submit proposed criteria to sponsoring societies – October-November, 1998
· Collect a series of interpretive documents that explain how specific programs comply with the developed criteria.
· Review existing programs to establish the direction that the model program effort should now take.
· Develop initial guidelines for accreditation of programs at the level of the software technician and graduate programs in the field.

Milestones

M9 Criteria submitted to societies – Target November, 1998
M10 All other tasks complete – Target Date January 1, 2000
