ITS TIME TO GET BACK TO WORK :-)

My overall feeling is that if we are getting the high levels of approval that we are, we should:

1) be wary of making changes that some who currently approve of the code might disapprove of;

2) be wary of major new develpments such as stating the principles in a 'stand alone' manner.

I presume from your comments that NONE of the clauses had majority disapproval.

*SIZE

One of the common complaints is the size of the code.

I think 5.02 could be removed as being too technical.

Following the ACM model. I suggest that we take the Eight keyword

Principles and expand then by a word or three so that they could stand on their own.

As expressed above, I am wary of this, but think it would be OK **provided** we make it clear that they are only extracted from the full code, and should never be used on their own, or quoted without a reminder that they are extracted from the full code.

Everyone?

---- WE NEED TO WORK ON THAT NOW - MODIFICATIONS OF - THE 8 PRINCIPLES.

I would prefer them to be perfectly unaltered, *including* the part sentences starting "In particular..." which help make the principles abundently clear as being only a short version of the whole code.

If they must be changed...

Principle 1: PRODUCT.

Software engineers shall, insofar as possible, ensure that the software on which they work is useful and of acceptable quality to the public (including by respecting privacy and being ethically, economically, culturally and environmentally sensitive), the employer, the client, and the user; completed on time and at reasonable cost; and free of error. In particular, software engineers shall, as appropriate ...

Principle 2: PUBLIC

Software engineers shall, in their professional role, act only in ways consistent with public and environmental safety, health and welfare, and is sensitive to the differences between people. In particular, software engineers shall ...

Principle 3: JUDGMENT

Software engineers shall, insofar as possible and consistent with Principle 2, protect both the independence of their professional judgment and their reputation for such judgment. In particular, software engineers shall, as appropriate ... (ie as was)

PRINCIPLE 4: CLIENT AND EMPLOYER.

Software engineers shall, consistent with the public health, safety, and welfare, always act in professional matters as faithful agents and trustees of their client or employer, including by identifying, documenting, and reporting to them problems or matters of social concern in the software. In particular, software engineers shall...

Principle 5 MANAGEMENT.

A software engineer in a management or leadership capacity shall act fairly and shall enable and encourage those who they lead to meet their own and collective obligations, including those under this code. In particular, those software engineers in leadership roles shall as appropriate... (ie as was)

Principle 6: PROFESSION.

Software engineers shall, in all professional matters, advance both the integrity and reputation of their profession as is consistent with public health, safety, and welfare, including by promoting honesty. In particular, software engineers shall, insofar as possible...

Principle 7: COLLEAGUES.

Software engineers shall treat all those with whom they work fairly, take positive steps to support collegial activities, and call on others for assistance when needed. In particular, software engineers shall, as appropriate...

Principle 8: SELF.

Software engineers shall, throughout their career, strive to enhance their own ability to practice their profession as it should be practiced, and support this code. In particular, software engineers shall continually endeavor to...

*TITLE- Code of Software Engineering Ethics and Professional Practices.is fine by me.

TESTING VERSUS GOOD DESIGN Where are there places where we might balance out the emphasis on good testing with elements of quality design? We should be able to add a word or two to a clauses and add this element.

GOOD DESIGN IS _IMPLICIT_ IN MANY OF THE EXISTING CLAUSES (eg 1.01-1.08 inclusive, 1.10 1.13-1.15, 2.05, 2.07, 2.09, 3.03, 3.04, 3.06?3.08, 4.01, 4.06-4.08, 5.03, etc, 8.01?8.02, 8.04 etc - On any or all of these, the words "As a contribution to good design of software" could be added at the start. 1.01-1.05, 1.15, 8.01?8.02, 8.04 are *more* appropriate for this treatment than the others, if it is needed to make it more explicit - some of the others could loose some of their meaning if the phrase were added).

1.0x * Refuse to work for or approve projects where the working conditions under which the project was produced are, in the engineer's opinion, likely to lead to unacceptably high risks to the public or the client's safety.[40a]

Looks like a good addition, now I have slightly modified the ending.

1.07. Ensure realistic estimates of cost, scheduling, personnel, and outcome on any project on which they work or propose to work and provide a risk assessment of these estimates I like the idea of replacing with: "only " take part in projects with the time and resources assigned to make it possible to do a good job."

[4i] 1.11. Be careful to use only accurate data derived from legal INSERT and ethical sources, and use only in ways properly authorized.

1.12. Whenever appropriate, delete outdated or flawed data REMOVE, OR REPLACE WITH "Promote understanding and awareness of the quality of the data management issues that surround the data that affect the product." [10i]

1.14. Promote maximum quality and minimum cost to the employer, the client, the user and the public. Make any tradeoffs clear to all parties concerned. 1.14 INSERT IN 2ND SENTENCE: "significant" [2i]

1.15. Work to follow industry standards that are most appropriate > for the task at hand, departing from these only when technically > justified

drop the word "technically" [2i]

2.05. Endeavor to produce software that respects diversity.

Issues of language, different abilities, physical access, mental access, economic advantage, and allocation of resources should all be considered

2.05 The specific text of clause 2.05 contains a puzzling term " mental access," as opposed to (?) Or (?) physical access."

See earlier email.

Today "diversity" is used to cover approval of actions with which I strongly disagree. How about the more neutral "differences"? [42a]

I could cope with this change, but would have to think very hard about attatching my name to the code if 2.05 were deleted.

3.08. Temper all technical judgements by the need to support and maintain human values.

3.08 I prefer "good" or "uplifting" values? [42a]

4.04. Not knowingly use illegally obtained or retained software.

I prefer ADDING

4.04 "unethically"[13a] So 4.04. Not knowingly use software that has been obtained or retained by illegal or unethical means.

5.0x The following looks like a worthy addition.

An additional item that might be needed is to: Ensure that coders are given, in sufficient quantities, time and resources, to complete documentation. [20a]

Similarly: 5.0x * Not punish software engineers informing leadership of problems in the project (as mentioned in clause 4.07 of this code)

5.01. Ensure that employees are informed of standards before being held to them.

Improved by this re?wording:

"Ensure there is a fair and reasonable standard of performance, and ensure that employees are informed of them before being held to them."[42a]

6.07. Only accept remuneration appropriate to professional qualifications or experience.

I STRONGLY SUPPORT 6.07 AND WOULD ARGUE FOR ITS RETENTION. THE DIFFICULTIES LISTED ARE USUALLY ADDRESSED BY THE SUBTLETY OF THE WORDING.

7.02. Review the work of other software engineers, which is not in the public domain, only with their prior knowledge, provided this is consistent with public health, safety, and welfare. 7.02 SLIGHT IMPROVEMENT ON WORDING: "only with proper authorization" [42a]

7.08. Not undermine another software engineer's job prospects for one's own personal gain.

7.08 I am concerned about the wording here ?? note the survey sheet says "Do no pursue a job offered to a colleague" ?? if that sort of "agree not to compete" meaning is intended, then I oppose this.

COULD THAT WORDING ON THE SURVEY SHEET ACCOUNT FOR THE HIGH DISAPPROVAL, RATHER THAN ANY REAL WORRIES WITH THE REAL WORDING?

"Not undermine with false, distorted, or irrelevant information another SE's job prospects ..."? [42a] WOULD BE OK INSTEAD.

I hope all of these comments are a help rather than a hinderance.
