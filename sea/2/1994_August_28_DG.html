-- Meeting Report --

Joint IEEE-CS/ACM SEEPP Task Force

June 11, 1994 - Boston College, MA

Report by Bob Melford and Don Gotterbarn (Co-Chairs)

1.0 PURPOSE OF MEETING This meeting of the SEEPP task force was held to address two issues: o "On-Line Library"
o working group formation and Chairperson selection

2.0 ATTENDANCE

Joyce Currie Little, Don Gotterbarn, Michael McFarland, Bob Melford and Keith Miller were present. Steven Barber, Patrick Sullivan and Suzie Weisband attended by conference call.


3.0 ON-LINE LIBRARY
Task force members agreed on the importance of a repository for pertinent documents, information, and other knowledge resources that is available for access from the Internet. Many references were made to the expected need of the SEEPP working groups needing a common foundation of knowledge. An "on-line" electronic repository is considered the only practicable solution.

3.1 CONTENTS
Electronic copies of relevant Codes of Ethics, annotated bibliographies, course syllabi, case studies, relevant papers and other professions' models of professional behavior should be included. Library must be true to its title and freely accessible to all Internet users.

3.1.1 COPY RIGHTS
We expect that many of the items we will wish to include in the library will be under copy right. Written releases, permitting electronic retrieval, from each copy right holder will be necessary. Each individual contributing to the library (see SPECIFIC ITEMS, below) will have to provide appropriate releases before the library makes it available.

3.1.2 SPECIFIC ITEMS
Specific items cited for inclusion and the TF members charged with securing them and their copy right release (if needed) are listed below by topic.

3.1.2.1 NCERC/CompuServe Forum
The organizers of the National Computer Ethics and Responsibilities Campaign have asked the SEEPP task force to recommend items for posting to the ethics discussion forum operated by CompuServe. The "NCERC" column below identifies our recommendations. The NCERC organizers will have to resolve any copy right issues for their use.

> 1.0 SCOPE
> These standards shall document generally accepted > principles for identifying and resolving ethical conflicts > relevant to the discipline of software engineering. >
> Consideration shall be given to the responsibilities and
> obligations of the Software Engineer to peers and laypersons, > employer, customer(s), the profession and society/humanity. > Additionally, consideration shall be similarly given to the > obligations and responsibilities of these various entities > towards the Software Engineer. >
> 1.1 GLOBAL APPLICATION
> The design, development and application of software often > transcend national boundaries. These standards are intended to
> document the consensus of the global software engineering > community. These standards shall also document various > recommended practices and guidelines when no clear consensus can > be established. >
> 2.0 PURPOSE
> The purpose of the SEEPP task force is to document the > international consensus for minimally acceptable ethical > responsibilities and obligations of Software Engineers. >

> 3.1.4 EXPENSES
> Participation is voluntary. Each individual is responsible
> for all expenses incurred to support his/her participation.
>
>
> 3.2 "OFFICIAL" IEEE STANDARD
> Working groups will operate according to the procedures
> specified for IEEE Standards (see Governance, below). However,
> working groups will NOT be initially chartered by the IEEE
> Computer Society's Standards Activities Board. (If the working
> groups were so chartered, individuals in several countries would
> be required to receive their government's approval for
> participation.)
>
>
> 3.3 GOVERNANCE
> The conduct of each working group shall follow the same
> written procedures that govern internationally recognized IEEE
> Standards Activities. These procedures specify that prompt
> consideration should be given to the written views and
> objections of all participants. Objectors should be notified of
> the disposition of an objection and the reasons therefor.
> Unresolved objections and the associated reasons should be
> reported to the entire working group's membership. The
> conclusions of the group should be supported by a consensus of
> the membership. (This would imply a ballot on the final
> products, probably run as IEEE Draft Standards ballots are
> currently run.)
>
> 3.4 E-MAIL & MEETINGS
> Working group business is expected to be primarily conducted
> via e-mail and computer mediated conferencing. From time to
> time, a working group may also conduct face to face or
> teleconfrence meetings. These meetings should be conducted in a
> manner which facilitates attendance by as many members as
> possible.
>
> 4.0 INTERNET ACCESS
> Each working group member must have a valid Internet e-mail
> account. The member should also be prepared to obtain access to
> other Internet applications such as FTP, TELNET, Gopher, WAIS
> WWW/MOSAIC, etc.
>
> 5.0 COMPLETION
> Working groups shall deliver their product to the SEEPP task
> force before November 1995.
