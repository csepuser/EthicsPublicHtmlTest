Thank You for Your Interest Email from Gotterbarn to SEEPP Work Group: "Thank You for Your Interest" - February 16, 1995 (Level 2) E-mail

Dear SEEPP Volunteer:

I thank you for your interest in this important effort of the joint IEEE-CS/ACM Steering committee and the working group of professional competence. The work of our task force has been slow to start due to some of our unfamiliarity with this process. I do sincerely apologize for the delay in getting this working group started.

I hope you continue to be interested in this important effort. We are all volunteers in this effort and there was some delay in determining how we should proceed. As you know, the IEEE-CS/ACM joint steering committee on establishing software engineering as a profession divided the work into three major task forces: one defining the current minimal skill set required to be a software engineer, another defining how to educate people with these skills and keep them current in new and developing technologies. These two task forces are well underway. The third task force is on software engineering ethics and professional practices(SEEPP). The goals of the third task force are to establish standards of professional practice for applying these skills and to determine and model minimal levels of ethical commitment in the utilization of these skills.

The task of developing professional practices and standards is new to most of us. The SEEPP task force has divided our task up into several sub-tasks each of which is assigned to a working group. ( A good computer problem solving technique.) A problem that has caused some of the delay is the question of how to manage and organize the working group process. I have a plan.

I would like our working group to proceed initially in the following way. We do know that other organizations and professions have developed standards of practice. These standards vary in numerous ways. Some standards of practice are codified and become black letter law for anyone who is practicing a profession, while other standards of practice are primarily guidelines that help one to make decisions in difficult situations. I believe that our standards of practice should probably be a combination of both; providing some solid unequivocal minimal standards of practice and also provide some guidelines and principles for resolving ethical issues that arise in the practice of the profession. Thess guidelines are needed to help address new or unanticipated situations created by our changing technology and evolving environments.

In determining how to proceed, there are formal procedural guidelines used by the IEEE for the functioning of working groups. I have appended a preliminary draft of a version of those guidelines which we are modifying for the SEEP task force. Although these address several logistical matters, they do not provide a technique for approaching the process. I would like to initiate the work of this group in the following way.

First, I have set up an small email list for the members of the professional competence working group at East Tennessee State- ProfComp @ ETSU-east_tenn-st.edu. Our group can use this list to circulate all correspondence to each member of the group. I will put together a list of the current members and I will circulate it to all members of the group two weeks after this letter has been sent. If you do not want you name circulated on this list please contact me before March 5. The names on this list will not be circulated to any other organization nor will it be made available by me to any other group Anything mailed to the list will only be circulated to members of the working group. The list will be moderated by me to eliminate spamming and other irrelevant activities. . Second, I propose the following general outline for the development of our standards in professional competence. A. We need to determine the content and structure for our document. Some general standards are contained in the appended procedures. But we need to be more specific. To this end we need to gather and distribute other profession's standards of practice. These need to be distributed to and digested by each of us. INFORMATION GATHERING: Please send to the LIST:

1. names and addresses of organizations that you know have standards of professional practices.

2. your willingness to get copies of these standards (preferably electronic) for the list.

3. your willingness to write a short abstract of that standard, indicating the scope of application of the standard, means they use to update it, how practitioners are educated about that standard, and the methods of standards enforcement, if any. ANALYSIS AND SYNTHESIS: Given this information we should be able to abstract a model of professional practices for our use. We can also use these documents, along with our knowledge of the nature of the software engineering profession to formulate a statement of scope ( i.e., what it is we think we should be doing) and a structure in which to organize our suggestions and conclusions. A Scope addresses the kinds of things we want to include in our standards and it clearly states who is(are) the intended audience(s) for the standard (practitioners, educators, professional society officials...)

B. Once the model is clearly stated we can further subdivide the problem based on the model and ask for volunteers to write rough first drafts of various sections of the document. These first drafts can then be circulated and modified, deleted from, added to, altered, etc. The procedure I have in mind is that all drafts, documents, suggestions for items to include or exclude will be circulated to the entire working group (an electronic meeting)

C. We can then revisit questions of format and content and develop a single consistent document which we can present the SEEPP task force.

D. For the present our meetings will merely be electronic. As we progress, there are some national meeting that several of us will be at and we can gather in subgroups at those meetings. If it is the consensus of the group that we need to all discuss something together we can arrange a conference call. (These are hard to organize and expensive, so they should be minimized.)

This procedure is written in Jello. Any suggestions on this process will be gratefully accepted. Please send an email 'Hi' to me at ProfComp, so that I know this message was received and that the list is working.

We are underway ! Please refer to item A. above.

Hoping for a productive relationship and grateful for your interest,

don gotterbarn

Joint IEEE-CS/ACM Software Engineering Ethics and Professional Practices (SEEPP) Task Force

Standards Development Working Group Guide to Operations

Introduction

This "Guide to Operations" is intended to help SEEPP task force (SEEPP TF) working group Chairperson's manage the production of their draft standard's document(s). These standards documents are to be developed in a manner consistent with the policies and procedures that govern the development official IEEE Standards.

The SEEPP TF has been jointly established on behalf of the IEEE Computer Society (IEEE-CS) and the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM). This document attempts to distill the relevant IEEE Standards policies, procedures and practices applicable to this joint entity.

This guide is derived from the IEEE and IEEE-CS Standards documents listed below in clause 2. Differences between this document and the official IEEE and IEEE-CS documents reflect the SEEPP TF's unique environmental constraints.

-ii- 1. Overview

This guide discusses how to manage the development of draft IEEE- CS/ACM standards for software engineering ethical and professional practices. While these draft standards are not being formulated under the auspices of a formal IEEE Standards Project Authorization Request (commonly known as a "PAR"), these standards documents are to be developed in accordance with appropriate IEEE and Computer Society Standard's Policies and Procedures.

The IEEE standards development process includes two volunteer groups: a Sponsor such as the SEEPP task force, who supervises all phases of the development of a standard, and a standards developing committee such as a Working Group (WG), which is responsible to the Sponsor, and which develops the draft standard. A SEEPP draft standard will be forwarded to the IEEE Computer Society Board of Governors and the ACM Council for action once it has met IEEE balloting approval criteria (see below).

1.1 Scope

This guide is intended for use by the SEEPP task force, its WG Chairpersons and members.

3. Definitions

3.1 IEEE Standards

IEEE Standards include but are not limited to

-- Standards: documents with mandatory requirements. (Mandatory requirements are generally characterized by use of the verb "shall.") -- Recommended practices: documents in which procedures and positions preferred by the IEEE are presented. (Recommended practices normally use the verb "should.") -- Guides: documents in which alternative approaches to good practice are suggested but no clear-cut recommendations are made. -- Trial-use documents: publications that are effective for not more than two years. They can be any of the categories of standards publications listed above.

IEEE Standards are signified by the use of the upper-case "S" in the word "Standards." (IEEE Standards activities operate under the umbrella of the American National Standards Institute (ANSI) as one of several accredited standards developing organizations. ANSI is a member entity of the International Organization for Standardization (ISO).)

3.2 IEEE-CS/ACM SEEPP draft standards

SEEPP draft standards include but are not limited to

-- standards: documents with mandatory requirements. (Mandatory requirements are generally characterized by use of the verb "shall.") -- recommended practices: documents in which procedures and positions preferred by the IEEE are presented. (Recommended practices normally use the verb "should.") -- guides: documents in which alternative approaches to good practice are suggested but no clear-cut recommendations are made. -- trial-use documents: publications that are -2- effective for not more than two years. They can be any of the categories of standards publications listed above.

The draft standards developed by the SEEPP WG's are signified by the using the lower-case "s" in the word "standard," whenever grammatically permitted. This convention shall be followed to distinguish SEEPP standards from approved IEEE Standards.

3.3 "Standard(s)"

The independent use of the word "standard," or its plural (i.e., without a leading 'IEEE' or 'IEEE-CS/ACM SEEPP') may be used generically in reference to standards, recommended practices, and guide documents, based on context. This is consistent with IEEE Standards practice and this guide's definition 3.1.

3.4 Acronyms

ACM: Association for Computing Machinery ANSI: American National Standards Institute BoG: Board of Governors CS: IEEE Computer Society IEEE-CS: IEEE Computer Society IEEE: Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers JtStr: Joint IEEE-CS/ACM Steering Committee OR: Organizational Representative P&P: Policies and Procedures RROO: Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised SAB: IEEE-CS Standards Activities Board SEEPP: Software Engineering Ethics and Professional Practices SEEPP TF: SEEPP task force WG: Working Group

4. Working Groups

Working groups (WGs) exist to organize the technical development of a draft standard.

4.1 Management & operation

4.1.1 Meetings

Working group meetings shall be held in a manner and with a frequency appropriate to facilitate the expeditious delivery of a draft standard appropriate for delivery to the IEEE-CS BoG and ACM Council. Meetings may be conducted in person, by IT-facilitate methods (e.g., e-mail), or in combination. Meeting dates, times, methods (technology), locations and frequency shall be determined by the WG Chairperson.

-3- 4.1.2 Quorum ÕIEEE-CS SAB P&Psþ

The default quorum for WGs shall be one-half (50%) of the voting membership. ÕHowever, see the discussion of quorum in RROO.þ

4.1.3 Voting/consensus

During the draft standard development cycle, WG Chairs have the discretion to use as expeditious a method as needed to establish the WG membership's consensus on any issue. In extreme cases, a formal vote of the WG membership may be necessary. Robert's Rules of Order (RROO) should be invoked to govern any fractious debate or vote.

4.1.4 The first draft

After establishing a draft Scope, (see clause 4.1.4.1.), the WG should outline the important issues and sub-topics it thinks the standard should address (see clause 4.1.4.2). The WG should also establish the nature of the standard it is to develop (i.e., standard, recommend practice, guide, etc., see 4.1.4.3).

Although it does not have to be included in the text of the standard, the WG should clearly and concisely define the purpose of the document. This generally describes "why" the document is being developed. For example:

"Purpose: Full-range current limiting fuses are not currently covered by standards. In fulfilling this need, the working group will also define and recommend an overall action plan to clarify and improve industry understanding for all types of fuses."

The WG Chairperson should then identify an individual to author an initial draft. The author should be permitted to prepare the draft with or without additional input or assistance from any other WG members, at his or her discretion. The consensus of the WG membership should then be evaluated to determine if the completed draft can subsequently serve as the foundation for an iterative process of refinement.

4.1.4.1 Scope

The WG must clearly and concisely define the scope of the document. The scope generally describes "what" will be affected, i.e., the technical boundaries of the document. For example:

"Scope: Communications equipment that is to operate in electric power generating stations and in generation, transmission, and distribution substations."

"Scope: Interface systems used to interconnect both programmable and nonprogrammable electronic measuring apparatus with other apparatus and accessories necessary -4- to assemble instrumentation systems."

"Scope: Peer-review panels or committees used to assess a Software Engineer's, or a Software Engineering Organization's conduct with respect to avoiding or resolving ethical conflicts."

4.1.4.2 Outline ÕIEEE Guide to Standards Developmentþ

The outline should be consistent with the requirements of the IEEE Standards Style Manual (see clause 5. in this guide).

Two outline styles are used for standards. Each WG should be aware of the advantages and disadvantages of both styles.

1) The first is that of the standard that states only requirements. This type of standard may then be followed by a guide, which provides guidance, examples, tutorial material, etc. The advantage of this approach is that it permits the scope of each effort to be restricted to a finite task, allowing each effort to be completed relatively quickly. 2) The second is the standard that incorporates guidance or tutorial material or both, in addition to requirements. This has the advantage of adding clarity to the standard. The disadvantage of this approach is that it may preclude the standard from being completed in a timely manner. Due to the amount of material being reviewed, it may take so long from the start of the project to its conclusion that all concerned may loose interest and leave.

4.1.4.3 Appropriate title

A title should include the type of document. For example:

a) *Standard* Policies and Procedures for Establishing and Operating an Ethics Peer-review Panel for Software Engineering Activities. b) *Recommended Practices* for Resolving Ethical Conflict ...

The title should not contain the words "IEEE" or "ACM."

4.1.5 Resolving changes

The WG may use the methodology discussed in clause 4.3.6, below.

4.2 WG Membership

4.2.1 Requirements

The default requirement for WG membership is a direct and material interest and willingness to participate in the activities of the WG, and attendance of at least two (2) out of last three (3) regularly-scheduled WG meetings, except in the case of newly- -5- formed WGs, for which voting privileges shall accrue to all eligible attendees at the initial meeting.

WG members shall participate in a manner consistent with their professional expert opinion as individuals, or as an organizational representative, but not both.

4.2.2 Expiration/Revocation ÕIEEE-CS SAB P&P'sþ

Membership for WG members (other than Officers) shall by default expire upon the third (3rd) consecutive regularly-scheduled meeting of the WG not attended by the member, unless there is good cause for an absence.

ÕA notice should be sent by the WG Chair to the member whose membership status is at risk thirty (30) days in advance of the meeting at which the membership would expire.þ

Membership shall be revoked upon the second consecutive WG ballot not returned in advance of the deadline, provided however that a warning notice to that effect was sent to the member, whose membership is subject to revocation, a minimum of thirty (30) days in advance of the deadline of the second WG ballot.

4.2.3 Elections of Chairs and Vice-Chairs ÕIEEE-CS SAB P&P'sþ

WG Chairs and Vice-Chairs shall be elected by the WG and confirmed by the SEEPP TF. The default and maximum term for Chair and Vice- Chair is two (2) years.

Initial appointments, and temporary appointments to fill vacancies due to resignations or removals may be made by the Co-Chairs of the SEEPP TF, and shall be valid for no longer than six (6) months.

Chairs and Vice-Chairs may be re-elected up to four (4) times.

4.2.3.1 Removal of Chairs or Vice-Chairs ÕIEEE-CS SAB P&P'sþ

The SEEPP TF may remove the Chair or Vice-Chair of a WG for cause.

The Co-Chairs of the SEEPP TF shall give the individual subject to removal a minimum of thirty (30) days written mail notice, with proof of delivery, of a meeting of the SEEPP TF at which the removal is to be decided. Such notice shall include the specific reasons for the proposed removal. The individual subject to removal shall have the opportunity to confront the evidence for removal, and to argue in his or her behalf.

In the clear and documented case of gross misconduct, the Chair of the SEEPP TF may suspend the Chair of the WG, with the concurrence of the Chair of the Joint IEEE Computer Society/ACM Steering Committee for Establishing Software Engineering as a

-6- Profession. A meeting or teleconference of the SEEPP TF shall be convened as soon as practical, but in no case later than thirty (30) days, to review the suspension as provided for above.

4.3 Balloting

Balloting shall provide for the development of consensus by all interests significantly affected by the scope of the standard. This is achieved by establishing a balloting group (or body) membership that balances such interests. Balance is defined as the avoidance of dominance by any single interest category ÕIEEE Standards Operations Manual, 5.4.1þ

The balloting body is composed of WG voting members and all other appropriate individuals and organizational representatives.

4.3.1 Process

The balloting process consists of the following steps:

Step responsible oversight ------------------------------- ----------- ------------

(1) Approval to ballot WG SEEPP TF

(2) Invitation to ballot issued SEEPP TF N/A

(3) Creation of balloting body SEEPP TF N/A

(4) Ballot SEEPP TF N/A

(5) Ballot resolution WG SEEPP TF

(6) Approval to forward to CS BoG/ACM Council WG/SEEPP TF JtStr

4.3.2 Balloting Body

The WG Chair or Vice-Chair shall request that the SEEPP task force form a balloting body for the purpose of conducting the ballot.

The balloting body for a standard is comprised of all individuals or organizational representatives (OR) who have expressed a direct and material interest in the scope of the proposed standard.

Those returning a request to the SEEPP TF to be included in the balloting body should indicate their interest category (see 4.3.4), and their agreement to return the ballot form and their comments within ten (10) days of receipt of the ballot.

The SEEPP TF Co-chairs' are responsible for ensuring that the balloting body meets the requirements of balance at the time the balloting body is formed, as described in 4.3.3 or in the IEEE Standards Operations Manual, and that there is no attempt to dominate the ballot by a special interest group.

4.3.3 Balance

The standard's balloting body shall be balanced by classifying the

-7- relationship of each member of the balloting body relative to the scope of the target standard. Standards balloting bodies shall consist of no more than one-half (50%) of participants from any category except "general." Organizational representatives may elect to ballot as the OR, or as an individual, but not both.

4.3.4 Interest categories

Balloting interest categories shall be established by the WG relative to a standard's scope. The SEEPP TF shall approve the balloting interest categories prior to issuing a invitation to ballot. By default, the following interest categories will be used: individual professional, academic, organizational representative, or end user/general interest.

4.3.5 The ballot Õ1993 IEEE Standards Operations Manual, 5.4.3.1þ

The ballot shall provide three choices:

a) Approve (Affirmative): This vote may be accompanied by comments suggesting corrections and improvements. Action on such comments is left to the discretion of the SEEPP task force. b) Do Not Approve (Negative): This vote shall be accompanied by specific reasons in sufficient detail so that the specific wording of the changes that will cause the negative voter to change his or her vote to "approve" can be readily be determined. If the changes are made, then the vote automatically becomes affirmative. In the absence of reasons for a negative vote, the ballot shall, after a follow-up inquiry, be classified as "no response." c) Abstain: This category is provided to allow for ballot returns from members who do not wish to review documents because of conflict of interest, lack of expertise, or other reasons. A reason shall be given for this vote; otherwise, the ballot shall be classified as "no response."

For a standards ballot to be effective, at least 75% of the ballots shall be returned. In the event that the 75% return from the balloting group cannot be obtained, the balloting process is considered to have failed. Further disposition of the document shall be the responsibility of the SEEPP TF. A minimum of 75% of those voting affirmative or negative must approve the draft in order to submit the draft to the IEEE-CS BoG and the ACM Council. In the event that 30% or more of the ballots are abstentions, the ballot shall be considered invalid.

The SEEPP TF is only obligated to consider ballots that are received by the close of the ballot. The ballot shall close at the end of business on the date specified on the ballot or when a 75% return of the balloting group is received. This extension for receipt of a 75% return shall be no longer than 60 days. -8- 4.3.6 Resolution of comments, objections, and negative votes Õ1993 IEEE Standards Operations Manual, 5.4.3.2þ

The SEEPP TF shall review all ballots received and shall make every attempt to resolve comments, objections, and negative votes favorably. Comments pointing out obvious mistakes, typographical errors, and improvements in punctuation, grammar, and composition, which do not change the technical meaning, may be accepted, revised, or rejected. ÕIt must be borne in mind that documents are professionally edited prior to Õfinalþ publication.þ

Comments accompanying affirmative votes that advocate changes in the technical meaning of the document may be accepted, revised, or rejected.

Changes may be made in the document to resolve negative votes or for other reasons. If the negative vote is not satisfied, either entirely or in part, the negative voter shall be informed of the reasons for the rejection and be given an opportunity either to change his or her vote to "approve" or to retain his or her negative vote.

All substantive technical changes made in the final draft to resolve comments, objections, and negative votes, and all unresolved negative votes, together with the reasons of the negative voter and the rebuttal by the WG members conducting the resolution of the ballots, shall be submitted to the balloting group, providing each member an opportunity to change his or her ballot. Names of the unresolved negative balloters are to be included with their negative comments in the recirculation of negative comments. Further resolution efforts may be required if additional negative votes result.

Proposed draft standards receiving a significant number of unresolved negative votes should be considered by the SEEPP TF for trial-use (see IEEE Standards Operations Manual, clause 5.7)

5. Style

The IEEE Standards Style Manual is the definitive source for guidance in formatting draft standards documents. The Style Manual also describes the optional and required contents of the drafts. Additionally, as the Style Guide notes in its introduction:

"In some cases, it is necessary and appropriate to accommodate special requirements. Standards developers should keep in mind that flexibility is allowed, and that IEEE Standards Project Editors are available to assist working groups in designing drafts that will suite their particular requirements."

-9- Of particular interest to the SEEPP TF working groups will be the Style Manual's clauses 5 and 6: "Content of drafts," and "General style," respectively.

Generally, standards documents are divided into four or more clauses, as follows:

Clause Title/subject ------ ------------------------------------------

1. Introduction

2. Scope

3. Definitions

4. Main text of the standard

4+n Additional text of the standard (optional)
