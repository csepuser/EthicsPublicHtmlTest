NEED TO ADD- COMPANIES WHO APPROVE AND TEXTS which use.

The draft evolved after extensive study of several computing and engineering codes[1]. All these codes try to educate and inspire the members of the professional group that adopts the code. Codes also informs the public about the responsibilities that are important to a profession. Codes instruct practitioners about the standards that society expects them to meet, and what their peers strive for and expect of each other. Codes are not meant to encourage litigation, and they are not legislation; but they do offer practical advice about issues that matter to professionals and their clients and they do inform policy makers. Based on the feedback from readers of this publication and from other sources, a final draft of the code will be developed and presented to the Steering Committee for approval.

Leonard,

Here is the best I can come up with as a history of our task force. There was significant time wasted waiting for approval of the process. The real work got done by a very small subset of the original responses to the CFP. The real work on the Code was done from July 1996- December 1997. From December 1997 until now most of the work has been done by the executive committee of the task force; tweaking the Code, responding to formal and informal reviews and nursing the Code through the approval process. I imagine many of the volunteers had no idea of the amount of work that would be involved. The timing of my sabbatical was very fortunate. The Code and supporting documents was the major product of my sabbatical at the Centre Computing and Social Responsibility. The work of Miller and Rogerson was essential to the completion of this project.

1 Gotterbarn Reorganized into a single committee consisting of the leaders of 7 of the 8 working groups and members of Gotterbarn's working group who had been active in developing the rough draft.
