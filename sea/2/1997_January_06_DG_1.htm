Michael,

Here is the letter. All this stuff at the top of the document below says- I had to send the message from England to ETSU to use the account that allowed me to send messages to the List server at the University of Tennessee Knoxville and then have the University of Tennessee send things to the list (( an aside- since we are still in business as a task force , it would probably be better if you do not mention the list's specific address in your paper.)

In the attached letter, Felipe is the IEEE-CS chair of the Steering Committee and Dennis is the ACM chair of the committee. The plan they refer to was sent from England in January or early February (you should have a copy--it is with those three large pacakges I sent) which has specific dates for deliverables from the task force to the Steering Committee and from the Steering Committee to the task force. You can use this plan and copies of material you have to see how the Task Force met its deliverable dates.

The technical confusion- someone else graciously let us use their list server. As E-mail addresses change- your identity changes (a Humean Problem:-) ) and you get denied access to a list. As my E-mail address changes, I too get denied access. Since I am the guy empowered to change E-mail addresses on the list and my identity has changed, I need to E-mail the systems managers at the University of Tennessee to change things. The saga continues .. I went on leave - Summer & Fall 1996 I was at George Washington University- it took two months before they had me on E-mail so I was using a commercial E-mail address for a while- need to notify Univ of Tennessee. The changed it to George Washington University address. I have to recover the date, but it was shortly thereafter that Felipe and Dennis sent the letter below. I then went off to Leicester England on January 3rd. Three days later I was on E-mail in England sending the letter below.

The current state of affairs, as best I understand it, is that comments on version 3.0 are arriving via fax, mail, and E-mail. The SEEPP executive committee is going to meet (face to face, in the same city) on December 4-6 to organize a response to the comments and a possible revision of the Code. This will be circulated to the task force and after one more go round the Code will be sent to the Steering Committee as a finished product.

Hope this helps give you more of the picture.

Cheers,

don

From: Donald Gotterbarn <dgot@dmu.ac.uk> Subject: Forwarded mail....

To: Multiple recipients of list PRFCMP-L <PRFCMP-L@UTKVM1.UTK.EDU> Status: RO X-Status:

Greetings, There have been some changes in the SEEPP task force you should be made aware of. I have been asked to direct and re-organize the efforts of our task force.( see attached memo) There are several items assigned to us by the ACM/IEEE-CS Steering Committee which have a short deadline. In order to address these tasks I have formed a combined working group collected under a single E-mail list called prfcmp-l. This explains why several of you have received E-mail saying you were added to this list. Please let me know if there are others who should be added to the list. Tomorrow I will send you a copy of the plan, I sent to the Steering Committee along with the draft code of ethics. I hope to have a draft statement of the roles and functions of professional codes of ethics to you all by Thursday. I am now working at The Centre for Computing and Social Responsibility, DeMontfort University and can be reached at dgot@dmu.ac.uk.

To correspond with the entire group simply send mail to prfcmp-l@utkvm1.utk.edu or just reply to this note. Do not send
individual mail to this list address.

More tomorrow .

regards,

don gotterbarn
