------------------- start quote ------------

TASK FORCE TO STUDY SOFTWARE ENGINEERING

The IEEE Computer Society and the Association for Computer Machinery, the world's two largest organizations of computing professionals, have established a Joint Steering Committee for the Establishment of Software Engineering as a Profession. This Steering Committee has established three task forces to acomplish its mission.

One task force is identifying the body of knowledge and recommended practices, and another task force is addressing educational issues. The task force on Software Engineering Ethics and Professional Practices (SEEPP) is charged with documenting and codifying standards of ethical and professional practices for software engineers.

The purpose of the SEEPP task force is to document the international consensus for minimally acceptable ethical/professional responsibilities and obligations of software engineers.

Participation is invited in this task force. You do not need to be a member of either society in order to participate.

Consideration shall be given to the responsibilities and obligations of the software engineer peers and lay persons, employers, customer(s), the profession, and society/humanity. Additionally, consideration shall be similarly given to the obligations and responsibilities of these various entities towards the software engineer.

The SEEPP task force recognizes that the design, development, and application of software often transcends national boundaries. They feel iy is vitally important that the global computing community have the means to ensure that this project produces a product which is as reflective of the global computing community's wisdom as can be reasonably achieved. The participation of individuals from around the world is important to acheiving this goal.

Participation is open to all individuals who are directly and meterially affected or interested in these issues. members of the international computing community are particularly encouraged to participate.
