Archive of emails Email from Gotterbarn to Davis and Anoop Kabra: "Archive of emails" - September 29, 2002 (Level 2) Electronic Mail

Michael and Anoop,
The historical outline below indicates possible hyperlinks in the archive of emails and documents. The emails and the documents should be hyperlinked.

There is a historical structure folder which has some other attempts to write a history. As you review them please coordinate them with this history. And as you go through other documents update this master history and confirm the dates. When you make changes or additions to this please update the version number.
Don

1993
Fletcher Buckley’s emails about registering Software Engineers-

May- ISEC Conference and IEEE-CS Technical Committee on Software Engineering
IEEE-CS Board of Governors meeting sets up a blue ribbon committee to consider the points made in Buckely’s memo
? – Statement by Blue ribbon committee to form 3 taskforces – Skills, Education, Ethics-
This is printed many places

1994
January 29th Melford and Gotterbarn meet with Mario Barbacci (head of Blue ribbon Committee at that time?) at Carnegie Mellon University to set up the ethics committee.
April
Nashville ACM meeting- met with Melford
30 Met in Washington- Deal with Format. Still following IEEE standards format- Buckley and Tripp as part of conference call.
May
Met with Steering committee in Pittsburgh- one topic was the press release about the congressional briefing in June.
June
June 11 Meeting at Boston College monastery with Mike McFarland – set up the 8 working groups
June 21 Congressional briefing

Sent draft CSP to task force for comment and return by 9-15, targeting an October 15 release of CFP

2 documents 10-15 and 10-17 First Call for participation- Lists the working groups and their leaders- decided in June of 1994

Apology to Task force

1996
January
Melford Completes operations guidelines- IEEE standards model

Feb 1-2 Professional Awareness in Software Engineering-
Gotterbarn represent ACM about work of Task Force
Melford present paper on inevitability of licensing
Rogerson present paper on Project Management Ethics

17 Meeting at ACM CSC Conference- News given to Gotterbarn at conference that the ACM may back out of the process unless Gotterbarn presents a complete plan of development to ACM. Melford indicated some difficulty with his being related to the project. Similar indications from some ACM leadership. No one explained any more to Gotterbarn about this. .

Several Task Force working group leaders meet at the conference and develop and aggressive schedule. Melford not present at meeting. Schedule depends on approval for some funding from ACM. Schedule submitted but no response from ACM to funding request. Went forward anyway- see letter to Gotterbarn’s professional competence task force- “early 96 aggressive schedule”
