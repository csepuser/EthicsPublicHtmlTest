1. Introductions

This was the third meeting of the Software Engineering Coordinating Committee (SWECC), a joint activity of the ACM and the IEEE Computer Society (CS). Gerald Engel attended the first part of the meeting to report on the progress of the Software Engineering Education Project (SWEEP). Mark Ardis volunteered to prepare the minutes of the meeting (Action Item 3 1).

The minutes of the first SWECC meeting and of the second (teleconference) meeting were approved. '

2. Review of action items

The committee reviewed the list of action items, noting those that had been completed, and setting new completion dates for those that had not been completed.

3. ICSE Report

Mark Ardis and Doris Carver reported on SWECC related activity at the recent International Conference on Software Engineering (ICSE). David _ Notkin, chairman of ACM SIGSOFT,, reported the ACM's position on licensing of software professionals at the annual open SIGSOFT meeting. While the ACM supports the education activities of SWECC, it is not ready to support the preparation of licensing exams. This viewpoint provoked many of the attendees of the SIGSOFT meeting, so that nearly the entire meeting was spent discussing licensing.

Mark Ardis gave an overview of the Software Engineering Body of Knowledge (SWEBOK) project at a birds of a feather meeting arranged by Don Baggert. The SWEBOK project provided the slides for the meeting. Several attendees noted the web address of SWEBOK, and several expressed interest in participating as reviewers.

4. Education Project Report

Gerald Engel gave an informal report of progress on the Software Engineering Education Project(SWEEP). The project has been working on 3 models of programs in software engineering: undergraduate, graduate (masters)., and pre professional. The undergraduate model has been completed. The graduate model is almost done., and a draft will be sent to the sponsoring societies soon. The preprofessional model has had the least progress, but an initial draft is expected this fall.

Concern was raised about the integration of CSAB and ABET. The plan was for CSAB to become the lead entity for accreditation reviews of software engineering programs. However,, ABET seems to be waiting for more information from CSAB. It was agreed that SWECC needs to be proactive in helping this integration. (Action Item 3.2)

SWEEP is planning a 3 day workshop to build consensus for software engineering education. They expect to have 30-40 attendees, who would submit position statements in order to qualify for an invitation. Besides building consensus, the workshop is intended to produce useful educational materials, provide some assessment of existing programs, and include some practitioner interaction.

We discussed possible funding sources for the SWEEP workshop, which is expected to cost about $60,.000. The National Science Foundation (NSF) might be willing to pay for half if matching support could be obtained from someone else. One suggestion was to ask professional societies from other countries to support participants from their countries. For example, the National Research Council of Canada (NRC) might be asked to support Canadian participants.

SWEEP is coordinating its work with 2 other efforts: Curriculum 2001 (C2001) and the Software Engineering Ethics and Professional Practices Project (SEEPP). Members of SWEEP are participating in C2001. There was some confusion over the ACM position with respect to SWECC projects (Action Item 3 3).

Finally, SWEEP was concerned with a need for publicity. How will the software industry and the general public become aware of SWECC's and SWEEP's work (See notes on publicity later.)

5. Engineering Accreditation Commission

Don Baggert had reported concern with the process for selecting and training reviewers of software engineering programs. It appears that ABET considers the IEEE to be the lead entity for computer engineering and software engineering. (I missed the action item here someone was going to talk to Schwartz about something.) (Action Item 3 4)

6. Code of Ethics Poster

The Software Engineering Ethics and Professional Practices Project (SEEPP) asked for comments on a publicity poster. SWECC agreed that the content was okay,, and the presentation could be improved by consulting with a graphics artist.

7. SWEBOK Project Report

Robert Dupuis, Pierre Bourque., and Alain Abran reported progress of the Software Engineering Body of Knowledge Project (SWEBOK). The Guide to the Software Engineering Body of Knowledge consists of ten Knowledge Areas (KAs). Eight of these have been completed, with the remaining two expected by July 23., 1999. This puts the Stoneman phase of the project about one month behind schedule.

A few hundred reviewers have volunteered to review version 0.5 of the Guide. All of the KAs have enough reviewers, but some additional reviewers are still being sought to represent other points of view (e.g., standards communities). Once the review is complete and comments have been addressed version 0.7 will be published, probably in November.

It was agreed that SWECC should take advantage of the publication of version 0.7 of the Guide by announcing its availability (Action Items 3 5 and 3 6). This will ensure widespread review and may help increase publicity about the efforts of SWECC.

8. Related Activities

It was noted that besides the Texas Board of Professional Engineers,, other groups (especially in Canada) have been taking actions about licensing software engineers. It was agreed that SWECC needs to enter into a formal agreement for the exchange of material between SWECC and each of these other groups (Action Item 3 7).

9. Performance Norms

We discussed the creation of performance norms for software engineering. This activity would identify skills and knowledge needed by software professionals. Volunteers from industry and academia would participate in these definitions. It is likely that a proposal for a SWECC endorsed project to define performance norms will be proposed soon.

10. Guidelines for Projects and Proposals

Dennis Frailey presented a set of guidelines for projects and proposals for new projects. (See attachment 1) The guidelines were approved as a SWECC policy. They become effective July 13.,1999, and they will be reviewed June 30, 2001.

11. Proposal for a Database of Software Engineering Programs

We reviewed a proposal for an annual survey of international software engineering programs. (See attachment 2) The proposal was endorsed, subject to three conditions:
1. The project should define their criteria for including software engineering programs in the categories they track. (E.g., Is a master degree program included if it contains only one course in software engineering Are master degree programs included only if they have the title "Software Engineering" )
2. The project should describe how they intend to collect information from non North American programs.
3. The project should identify representatives who will report to CS and SIGSOFT. Dennis Frailey will communicate with the project proposers on the status of their proposal (Action Item 3 8).

12. FY2000 Budget

The FY2000 budget was approved as originally presented.

13. Process Assessment

We discussed the relationship between the Capability Maturity Model (CMM) of the Software Engineering Institute (SEI) and the process component of SWEBOK. Although there is no direct connection between the two, it is desirable to beverage the understanding of the software engineering process that each represents. Leonard Tripp will talk to Steve Cross about this relationship (Action Item 3 9). One possible interaction might be to include someone from the SEI as a reviewer of SWEBOK under the category of standards setting bodies.

14. Ironman Phase of SWEBOK

Pierre Bourque and Alain Abran presented the plans for the Ironman phase of the SWEBOK project. Much of this effort will be collecting and analyzing feedback from the Stoneman version of the Guide to Software Engineering Knowledge. The Guide will then be updated.

The SWEBOK team was asked if they would be interested in managing a performance norms project in parallel with the Ironman phase. They expressed interest., but asked to have more time to consider the commitment of resources.

We discussed possible funding of Ironman. The current Industrial Advisory Board would be asked it they wished to continue their sponsorship, and some new members would be sought.

Publication of the Stoneman version of the Guide will make it possible for authors to publish tutorials on its contents. We noted that short presentations of the Guide might be given by any member of SWECC, but longer presentations (e.g., half day tutorials) would require too much preparation. Tutorials and presentations would help increase awareness of SWEBOK within the software engineering community. We should look for opportunities to give such presentations.

15. Future Plans

Publicity for SWECC and its projects was discussed. One way to generate publicity would be to present the Stoneman Guide to the Body of Knowledge at conferences we attend. For example, the Software Technology Conference (STC) is attended by a large portion of the defense software industry. We might be able to arrange a session or multiple sessions on SWEBOK there. Other possible venues are ICSE in Ireland in 2000, FIE for educators, and the annual SEI meeting. Mark Ardis agreed to coordinate a list of conferences that SWECC members will attend (Action Item 3 10), so that we can take advantage of opportunities to present SWEBOK.

SWECC will meet again in late January 2000. If there is enough business there will be an interim teleconference meeting in October 1999.
