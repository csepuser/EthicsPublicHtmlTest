Trip to Washington Email from Gotterbarn to Mike Lichtenstein: "Trip to Washington" - May 26, 1994 (Level 2) E-mail

ACM
1515 Broadway
New York, New York 10036

Dear Mike Lichtenstein,

I am the ACM representative to a joint IEEE-CS/ACM task force. The ACM has agreed to cover my travel expenses up to $400 for travel related to this task force.

In response to the question about special forms, Stu Zweben sent the message below. I hope this itemization and the attached receipts are adequate. If there is anything else I need to do please let me know. Thanks for your help with this.

Sincerely,

Donald Gotterbarn

The task force had its first meeting in Washington DC on April 30th. I drove to that meeting, a distance of 1095 miles round trip.

There was another meeting in Pittsburgh on May 28th. The airfare was $163 and the car rental was $35. It is cheaper to rent a car at government rates than to take a taxi.

The House of Representatives briefing in Washington occurs on a date when I was already traveling. I was able to adjust my original ticket for an added cost of $60 so that I could stop in Washington. The cost of taxi from National airport to the Rayburn House office building is $11.00 each way.
