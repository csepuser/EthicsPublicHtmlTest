Notes from IEEE guy Peter Prinz- build some of this into the schedule. An interesting approach to "enforcement" when this is not the code of Ethics of Either Society. Quote from Peter Prinz " Good luck with the final stages of authorization and publication.

I'd suggest that the Society provide sessions at all of our Conferences and Tutorials to propagate the Code. Each member and non-IEEE member should be able to attend these sessions, and the Society should maintain a Registry of all individuals who have formerly agreed to follow the Code.

I do not think that the Registry would expose the Society or IEEE to any commercial risk, and should provide an positive venue for membership drives and sustaining the high standards of conduct for the Society. You might want to pass this by Michael Elliott, Society Director."

As can be seen by the schedule below, if completion by Dec 1997 means completion of the code which reflects a consensus of those concerned then it is a very tight schedule. Proposed Task Force Structure: A balanced code will address, the engineering, the management and the academic sides of software engineering. The structure of the task reflects that balance. Rather than partitioning the leadership along professional association membership lines, the executive committee represents three areas that need to be represented in the code. The previous organization of the task force divided the group along lines of ethical issues. This was not productive. The proposed structure of a panel of experts allows individual experts to contribute when their specialty is involved. The panel includes representatives from several professional societies including; the ACM, the IEEE-CS, the ICCP, the BCS, IDP and the Deutsche Gesellshaft Fur Informatik. It also includes practicing software engineers and ethics advisors for major, multinational corporations.

Executive Committee D. Gotterbarn Chair K. Miller, Software Engineering Coordinator S. Rogerson, Technical Management Coordinator
