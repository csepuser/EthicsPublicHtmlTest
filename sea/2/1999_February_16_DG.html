ACM Computer Science Conference in Philadelphia Email from Gotterbarn to Cabrera and Frailey: "ACM Computer Science Conference in Philadelphia" - February 16, 1999 (Level 2) E-mail

Dear Messrs. Cabrera and Frailey,

The SEEPP task force met on Saturday 16 February from 8-12 at the ACM Computer Science Conference in Philadelphia.

I began by reporting to the group the interest of the British Computer Society in our work and their desire to work with us, and expressing concern with our progress. I informed the task force that I am scheduled for sabbatical next year and the SEEPP project is one of the projects I will be working on. I have already scheduled work on it with task force members in the U.K. I presented a high level schedule for the completion of our task which would eliminate the episodic approach we had taken. That preliminary schedule is attached. In order to help define elements in our deliverable, I distributed a paper I wrote comparing the work of several professional societies.

The schedule was reviewed and most of the meeting was spent detailing and assigning specific tasks needed to complete the standards of practices, standards of conduct and standards of ethics preliminary document to be presented to the IEEE-CS/ACM steering committee in January 1997. The document is considered preliminary because we will need to incorporate and be responsive to the input of the skills task force and the education task force.

During the meeting, an optimistic biweekly schedule of deliverables was developed. I will personally see monthly deliverables from each of the working groups. We are all volunteers, but to be able to accomplish our task effectively and efficiently we will need some financial support. Through early June most of the work will be done using e-mail. E-mail, unfortunately, is a very sterile medium for the creative exchange of ideas. ( It is also very easy to procrastinate if E-mail is the only means of communication.)

We have 8 separate working groups and anticipate costs for 1 or 2 conference calls. As the materials from each group become formed, we will need to meet again. Our preliminary plans are to meet at the Brookings Institute in Washington, DC. Whatever support the two societies can give for that event will be greatly appreciated. We have tried to hold meetings in conjunction with other professional conferences. The organizations we work for would provide support for our attendance at these meetings. There is no Software Engineering, Computer Science or computer ethics meeting scheduled for early Fall that most of us would be attending.

After planning how to develop each working group's product, we devoted some of the meeting to planning how best to integrate the working group's products into a single well-formed document. We believe it is best to turn the results of the several groups over to a single skilled writer who, acting as a scribe, will be responsible for will be responsible for organizing the multiple documents into a single product. As we talked of this task, we realized that it would take several weeks of concerted effort from the scribe. As volunteers, we devote pieces of our time to the work of the professional societies. The task of the scribe is a full-time job for several weeks and we would like the societies, jointly, to provide an honorarium to the person who acts as scribe.

At the moment, the committee would like Professor Keith Miller to be the scribe. He is a practicing software engineer who has had contracts with NASA and published widely on software testing. He is also a university professor teaching computer science and working in the area of computer ethics. The plan we developed call for working groups to have completed preliminary materials by July 15th available for the scribe. The scribe would work on the documents and return them to the working groups for another iteration by August 15, Each working group would then edit and modify there document based on input from the working group members and return their documents through me ,to the scribe for further revision and integration. This process would go through one or two iterations.

The plan then calls for a meeting in Washington in October or November to have the group do a final run through of the document. Because of the location, we hope to involve one or two people from each of the societies boards in that review process. After further revision we plan to deliver the document to the steering committee. After their review, we would like to follow a process that was helpful in developing the ACM Code of ETHICS and Guidelines for Professional Conduct. Namely, we would like to submit the document to the membership for review and comment by publishing it in the Communications of the ACM and in IEEE Computer.

The comment process is facilitated by the inclusion of a turn-around document attached to the article in the magazines. Please let me know what you think of these plans. Sincerely, Don Gotterbarn Co-chair IEEE-CS/ACM Software Engineering Ethics and Professional Practices.

Preliminary Long Range Schedule DONE

1. Identified 8 areas of concern and formed working groups. (may add new areas as required.) NEW (February- December 1996)

2. For each working group: a. gather relevant standards of practice, standards of conduct, and critical ethical issues in the working groups area. b. from the responses, develop a draft standard for professional practice, in a code of practice and a code of conduct. c. circulate draft within groups, then within task force for comments. d. revise drafts in the light of comments.

3. (Scribe) Combine all groups drafts into a single version.

4. Present this as a preliminary document to the steering committee. NEW ( September 1996-January 1998)

5. Begin balloting on Preliminary draft-( 4. is a precondition- start target 2/1/97))

6. Begin development of a process for using these standards in decision making. (start target 9/15/96)

7. After revisions circulate both documents to industry for comment.

8. Revise process document. Dependent on input from skills task force

9. Examine and revise preliminary documents based on input from the Skills Task Force. NEW (January 1998-January 1999) Dependent on input from Education

10. Start to include work done by curriculum task force and curriculum models from other societies.

11. Revise all documents and submit for approval by steering committee.

12. Revise all documents and disband the SEEPP task force.
