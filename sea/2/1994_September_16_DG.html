>codes of ethics to me. OK, I've checked with the various "computer law associations" and they have no independent codes. They consider themselves educational organizations rather than professional groups (for tax purposes I bet) and therefore it would a little beyond their charter, or so they feel.

As far as the ABA Model Rules of Professional conduct, I haven't been able to get to get a hold of an electronic copy. But I'm close -- Cornell has a copy of the Idaho Rules as enacted on the World Wide Web at http://www.law.cornell.edu/lawyers/ruletable.html

What happens is that the ABA proposed the Model Rules, and then the individual states enact them into law as they see fit, usually with minor changes. I think this is close enough for SEEPP purposes, but please let me know if you feel differently.

> and since the weekend is approaching and there is no baseball to > watch, please cast an editorial eye at the CFP.

Ha! I'm off to Paris for 10 days tonight -- vacation. Dream on, Don-boy. No work is going with me.

I have actually cast a conceptual, if not an editorial, eye on the pieces of the CFP that were nearest to my heart and found nothing I couldn't live with.

-Steve
