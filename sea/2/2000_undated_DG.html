SEEPP FILES Timeline of SEEPP Task Force and SEEPP File List - undated, 2000 (Level 2) Electronic Document

SEEPP FILES

I. The Beginning- Identifying a need and how to address it. SEEPP was following an IEEE model of developing a standard. In retrospect this had two problems. One the process is very drawn out and depends on multiple groups of otherwise busy volunteers. The only SEEPP task force leader who knew anything about this process did not have enough time to devote to the project.

A. A need was identified 1993 *May IEEE-CS Board of Governors in response to Fletcher Buckley proposal- distributed earlier Profesisoalize SE *Fall ACM Separately forms a “Commission on Software Engineering to address a number of questions related to 1) the terminology used to describe software engineering and those who work in the software area; 2) identify generally accepted and desired standards of good software practice; and 3) determine our ability to identify, educate, and train individuals who are competent with software engineering and design.”

*ACM joins effort Ad hoc committee formed

Report to BOG from steering committee (11/12/93)- cite May meeting in Baltimore Meeting schedule plan from 1/29/94 Following IEEE Standards Board Project Authorization Request

1994---- 29 Jan ?? Don Gotterbarn and Bob Melford meet in Pittsburgh with Mario Barbacci and plan the approach to the ethics portion of the process. The IEEE working model was adopted. This model sets up a task force of working group leaders. The members of the working groups do the work and report back to the task force. At this meeting a schedule was laid out based on the delivery of a product that was not clearly defined, but the method would be to follow the IEEE model. Melford was the one familiar with this methodology and started to put together the Call For Participation which would comply with IEEE standards.

January 1994, the president of the IEEE-CS made a proposal to the ACM to form a Joint Steering Committee to accomplish these goals.

4/30 Washington Meeting of some SEEP task force members after CEI Meeting April 30, 1994 - plan to rely on multiple focus groups and surveys: List task force by roles- partial list of attendees.
