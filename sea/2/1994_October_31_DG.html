IMPORTANT ?? Please tell us about your specific interests and how your experience will aid this project.

I am concerned about the need to create Software Engineering as a Profession. I believe that present academic qualifications are insufficient to judge a persons competance in the computer industry. I do not have any academic qualifications, am lousy at maths, and still have managed to become MIS manager at one of Irelands largest PLCs. I am now techical director of the Trinity Group (Ireland second largest DEC reseller) and have just launched an electronic mail service company. Most of the people I know who have 3rd level qualifications and are working in the computer industry, do not hold their degrees in computer?related subjects.

I agree with the need for "formalise" all aspects of the computer profession, but I want to see real, industry?related testing. For instance, the skills required of a commercial programmer (writing business applications) are different from those of one writing an operating system. I have just employed a graduate who holds a degree, diploma, and certificate in computer science. When asked to plug a modem into a PC, she asked which port was the comm port? She had never had to do this on her courses. In a previous job, I employed a commercial programmer with no academic qualifications. She was programming *productively* in two days.
