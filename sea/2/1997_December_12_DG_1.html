Re: Version 4.0 of the Code Email from Miller to Rogerson and Gotterbarn: " Re: Version 4.0 of the Code - comments" - December 15, 1997 (Level 2) E-mail

Simon and Don:

My initial reaction to all of these comments are that we cannot do this forever, trying to please everyone over and over. At some point we have to say enough.

Having said that, if we can QUICKLY come to some language that is a clear improvement on our already improved language, so be it.

I'll chime in below, but only a few times.

Keith

On Fri, 12 Dec 1997, Simon Rogerson wrote:

> Do

> I THINK BEN'S CONCERN IS VALID AND SUGGEST WE ADD A SENTENCE THAT POINTS
> PEOPLE TO THE FULL CODE AND EXPLAINS THE IMPORTANCE OF THE CLAUSES

Suggestion for 2 such sentences: "The short version of the code gives the
aspirational elements at a high level of abstraction; the clauses that
follow give examples and details of how these aspirations change the way
we act as professionals. Without the asperations, the details can become
legalistic and tedious; without the details, the asperation can become
high sounding but empty; together, the aspirations and the details form a
cohesive code."


You two decide about "moderate" or "temperate." You can't please
everyone, and I'm tired of dancing around on this point.

>
> >
> > > 1.04. Disclose to appropriate persons or authorities any actual or
> > > potential danger to the user, the public, or the environment, that
> > > they reasonably believe to be associated with software or related
> > > documents.
> >
> > The deletion of "or merely know about" is detrimental, although the
> > deletion of the word 'merely', itself, is appropriate.
>
> NO I THINK BELIEVE INCLUDES KNOW ABOUT IN THIS CONTEXT
>

Put a cork in it, Ben. What we have here is good.

> >
> > > 1.07. Consider issues of physical disabilities, allocation of resources,
> > > economic disadvantage and other factors that can diminish
> > > access to the benefits of software.
> >
> > Loss of the mentions of language and 'mental access' is detrimental, even
> > if the earlier version was poorly expressed. I prefer:
> >
> > 1.07. Endeavour to produce software that respects diversity. Consider
> > issues including those of language, economic advantage, allocation of
> > resources, and different abilities (including how to make software usable
> > by people with physical and sensory impairments, learning difficulties,
> > and conditions such as epilepsy).
>
> WHILST I HAVE GREAT SYMPATHY WITH THIS I THINK IT BECOMES TOO LONG AND
> TOO DETAILED ? PERHAPS THIS IS AN AREA WHERE WE CAN DEVELOP SOME WORKED
> EXAMPLE
>

Or Ben can work up an example.

> >
> > > Principle 3 PRODUCT Software engineers shall ensure that their
> > > products and related modifications are of the highest professional
> > > standards possible. In particular, software engineers shall, as
> > > appropriate:
> >
> > The removal of detail improves the sentence. However, loss of the mention
> > of pruducts being *acceptable*, to to the employer, the client, the user
> > and the public creates a deficiency with the code: it should have become
> > a clause.
>
> IF THEY ARE OF THE HIGHEST PROFESIONAL STANDARDS THEN BY INFERNECE THEY
> WILL BE ACCEPTABLE AS THIS IS PART OF THE DEFINITION OF PROFESSIOANLISM.
> HOWEVER, THE IDEA OF AN EXTRA CLAUSE (OR ADDITION TO AN EXISTING CLAUSE)
> IS WORTH DOING
>

I disagree, but not violently. If you want to add yet another clause,
fine. I might point out, however, that you can work to the highest
standards and still have some idiot not "accept" your work.
Part of being a professional is knowing when to adhere to standards
when idiots demand something else.

> >
> > > 3.01. Strive for high quality, acceptable cost and a reasonable
> > > schedule, ensuring significant tradeoffs are clear to the
> > > employer, the client, the user and the public.
> >
> > Making tradeoffs clear is *not* sufficient to compensate for the loss of
> > the call to strive for high quality and acceptable cost for the employer,
> > client, user, and public.
> >
> > I also wonder if the wording is *accidentally* deficient in the way it
> > implies "employer, the client, the user and the public" only applies to
> > tradeoffs, not to "high quality, acceptable cost and a reasonable
> > schedule". What about re?ordering the clause to "3.01 For each of the
> > employer, client, user and the public: Strive for high quality, acceptable
> > cost and a reasonable schedule, ensuring significant tradeoffs are clear
> > to all parties concerned."
>
> THE REWORD IS WRONG WRT SENCE IN THAT IT IS S/W ENGS SHALL ...
>
> HOW ABOUT ADDING AFTER "TRADEOFFS ARE CLEAR TO" "AND ACCEPTED BY"
>

Simon's rewording is better, but remember my point from above.

> >
> >
> > > 3.09. Provide realistic estimates of cost, scheduling, personnel, and
> > > outcomes on any project on which they work or propose to
> > > work and provide a risk assessment of these estimates.
> >
> > It may not be a given software engineer's responsibility to *provide* the
> > estimates, but it is always the software engineer's responsiblity to

>
> I THINK A S/W ENG SHOULD NOT ALWAYS WAIT FOR A REQUEST FOR AN ESTIMATE
> BUT WHERE APPROPRIATE SHOULD BE FORTHCOMING WITHOUT PROMPTING ? SO REJECT

Change "Provide" to "Ensure." Enough said.
