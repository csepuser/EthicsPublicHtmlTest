Subject: Email from Frailey to Gotterbarn: "Re: Software Engineering code of Ethics and Professional Practice-Steering Committee Preparing a recommendation, concern over Professional Practices section"
Recepient: Don Gotterbarn
Format: Format
Source: Don Gotterbarn

Re: Software Engineering code of Ethics and Professional Practice Email from Frailey to Gotterbarn: "Re: Software Engineering code of Ethics and Professional Practice-Steering Committee Preparing a recommendation, concern over Professional Practices section" - April 8, 1998 (Level 2) E-mail

Don,

I wish you had contacted me before sending this. The steering committee is preparing a recommendation to the two bodies you mentioned.

I should point out to you that there is consensus, at least there appears to be, that the code of ethics portion is suitable for recommending. However there is some concern that the professional practices part is not ready for recommendation, mainly because some steering committee members feel the field is too immature to be recommending such practices. We are trying to work this out and plan to make a formal recommendation to the two councils in May.

I was waiting to contact you further until more members of the steering committee had participated in the discussion of the committee's final recommendations. But since you have send this, let me ask you one simple question.

Should the code of ethics activity continue and, if so, what bodies of ACM and the computer society should sponsor it? We are going to be recommending that each of the subactivities of the steering committee be continued, if appropriate, to be jointly sponsored by the appropriate bodies in the two societies, with a new "coordination" body to be formed as a replacement for the steering committee.

Regards,

DJF
