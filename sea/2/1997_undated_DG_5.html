Abstract:

The Professionalization of Software Engineering and its significance for Ethics Education

Two major computer societies, the IEEE-CS and the ACM, have jointly undertaken the responsibility of professionalizing software engineering. This work has been distributed amongst three task forces, one of which is addressing issues in software engineering ethics and professional practices. This task force, developing a code of ethics and standards of conduct, has a multinational membership; reflecting the make up of the two professional organizations. The code of ethics and standards of conduct developed by this group will have significance for the future development of software and the ethics education of software developers.

This paper discusses two of the differences between the approaches of various national communities to this project and the achievement of consensus between them. Several of the ethical questions are related to the relative position of rights based ethics versus virtue based ethics in the different cultures. This is reflected, for example, in different values placed on privacy. Other issues are related to the actual and acceptable role of governments in the software development process. This is reflected in different values or obligations related to data integrity.

The papers then presents some consequences such differences have for approaches to the ethics education of computer professionals.
