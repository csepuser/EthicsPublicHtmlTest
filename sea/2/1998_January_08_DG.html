Software Engineering Code of Ethics Email from Sigut to Gotterbarn: "Software Engineering Code of Ethics" - January 8, 1998 (Level 2) E-mail

I propose certain changes to the Software Engineering Code of Ethics, Version 3, as published by the IEEE-CS/ACM Joint Task Force on Software Engineering Ethics and Professional Practices. My proposal can be divided into three parts:

1 Changes in the form
2 Changes in the Priciples
3 Other changes and comments

1 Changes in the form
---------------------

I propose that the form be changed from 2 parts (Preamble and Principles) to 3 parts, namely Preamble, Principles and Clauses. This should mainly serve the separation of the Principles and the Clauses. In the current version these are combined, so that somebody wanting to consider the Principles on their own can NOT do so without a major effort.

In the new form the Preamble would stay as it is, the Principles would be separated (example see in Part 2 of this proposal) and the Clauses would have the following form:

CLAUSES

Principle N: PRINCIPLE_NAME

<Principle text as given in PRINCIPLES followed by>
In particular, software engineers shall:

aspire to:
N.01. Text of the clause...

...

be expected to:
N.xx. Text of the clause...

...

be demanded to:
N.yy. Text of the clause...

...

The new subheaders "aspire to" etc. should clarify the intent of the clauses as described in the Preamble.

2 Changes in the Priciples
--------------------------

This part should illustrate the form in which the "Principle" part of the Code could be given as well as present some changes I feel should be introduced.

First the form of the new "Principle" text:

PRINCIPLES

Principle 1: PROFESSION

Software engineers shall, in all professional matters, advance both the integrity and reputation of their profession.

Principle 2: SELF

Software engineers shall practice their professional work to the best of their knowledge and ability. Outside of professional engagements they should strive to live an integral life.

Principle 3: PUBLIC

Software engineers shall, in their professional role, act only in ways consistent with the public safety, health and welfare. This principle is implicitly included in all the other principles of this code.

Principle 4: PRODUCT

Software engineers shall, insofar as possible, ensure that the software on which they work is useful and of acceptable quality to the public, the employer, the client, and the user; completed on time and at reasonable cost; and free of error.

Principle 5: JUDGMENT

Software engineers shall, insofar as possible, protect both the independence of their professional judgment and their reputation for such judgment.

Principle 6: SELFIMPROVEMENT

Software engineers shall, throughout their career, strive to enhance their own ability to practice their profession as it should be practiced.

Principle 7: CLIENT AND EMPLOYER

Software engineers shall always act in professional matters as faithful agents and trustees of their client or employer.

Principle 8: COLLEAGUES

Software engineers shall treat all those with whom they work fairly and take positive steps to support collegial activities.

Principle 9: MANAGEMENT

A software engineer in a management, leadership or teaching capacity shall act fairly and shall enable and encourage those who they lead to meet their own and collective obligations, including those under this code.

You will notice that I reordered the Principles, as well as added an additional one. Let me start with the new Principle: I called it SELF, renaming at the same time the old SELF to (in my eyes more appropriate) SELFIMPROVEMENT. The reason for the new Principle is twofold. The first sentence is supposed to sum up the intent which lies more or less implicit in all the other Principles but is never explicitely stated. The second sentence should integrate the software engineer in the surrounding society which is otherwise unmentioned apart from 6.11. (All the Principles are talking about professional behaviour, leaving possibilities like stealing software privately a fully legal option).

I tried to reorder the Principles according to the importance I can read into them. Since the Code is a sort of a professional charter, I feel that the Profession should come first. The Self and the Public come next, as they are the most important actors. Then follows the subject and the factors influencing it followed by the other actors. Management comes at the end, because it does not necessarily concerns all software engineers.

Now to the Principles themselves. As a general feature I deleted the "public safety" etc. from all of them except Public and declared it (in Public) to be a general feature. This should help to make the Principles readable. Principle Self is new as described above. Selfimprovement
is the new name for old "Self" as described above. I extended the Management Principle to include teaching, because teaching is also a variant of a leadership role.

3 Other changes and comments
----------------------------

In addition to the two previous themes I have some comments on the text itself:

PREAMBLE

Point 1:

The Code contains eight Principles related to the behavior of and ... nine if you accept my new Principle

Point 2:

... second level obliges software engineering professionals to more challenging obligations than those required at level one. ...

perhaps

... second level obliges software engineering professionals to more specific obligations than those required at level one. ...

(some might percieve level one obligations to be more challenging!)

Point 3:

I would like to change

care to people affected by their work. The third and deeper level comprises several obligations which derive directly from elements unique to the professional practice of software engineering. The Clauses of each Principle are illustrations of the various levels of obligation included in that relationship.

The Clauses under each Principle consist of three different types of statement corresponding to each level. ...

to

care to people affected by their work. The third and deeper level comprises several obligations which derive directly from elements unique to the professional practice of software engineering.

The Clauses of each Principle are illustrations of the various levels of obligation included in that relationship. They include three different types of statement corresponding to each level. ...

That way the division between Principles and Clauses might be made clearer.


Point 4:

Although all three levels of professional obligation are recognized, perhaps better

Although three levels of professional obligation are recognized,

Point 5:

The list of Principles and Clauses is not exhaustive , perhaps better

The list of Clauses is not exhaustive,

(Principles are exhaustive by definition!)

Point 6:

which are ethically improper to request of an software engineer. a soft... ?

CLAUSES (numbering as in the original text)

Point 7:

1.03. Ensure that they are qualified, by an appropriate combination of education and experience, for any project on which they work or propose to work.

Willingness to learn should be included (compare 5.03)

Point 8:

1.11. Be careful to use only accurate data derived from legal sources, and use only in ways properly authorized. and use it only ...??

Point 9:

2.03 and 3.02 might be less redundant and better categorised as follows:

2.03. Affix their signature only to documents prepared under their supervision or within their areas of competence.

3.02 Affix their signature only to documents with which they are in agreement.

Point 10:

4.04. Not knowingly use illegally obtained or retained software.

"knowingly" might be superfluous.

Point 11:

4.05. Keep as confidential information gained in their professional work that is not in the public domain, where such confidentiality is not inconsistent with matters of public concern.

Should be probably worded similarly to:

4.09. ... , unless a higher ethical concern is being compromised; then in that case the employer or another appropriate authority should be informed of the engineer's ethical concern.

Point 12:

6.05. Report anything reasonably believed to be a violation of this code to appropriate authorities.

How about advising/confronting the (unwitting?!) violator first?
(cmp. also 5.04)

Point 13:

6.07. Only accept remuneration appropriate to professional qualifications or experience.

And if the results are far beyond it? (genius, lucky strike, ...)

Point 14:

6.11. Exercise professional responsibility to society by constructively serving in civic affairs.

I would classify that under the new Self, category "aspire to", perhaps even stretching the envelope a bit.

Point 15:

7.06. Assist colleagues in being fully aware of current standard work practices including policies and procedures for protecting passwords, files, security measures in general, and other confidential information.

perhaps

7.06. Assist colleagues in being fully aware of current standard work practices and security measures in general, as well as other confidential information.

because a) spelling out various security measures makes it unreadable and b) standard practices and security are different from conf.inf.

Point 16:

7.08. Not undermine another software engineer's job prospects for one's own personal gain.

Does it mean I cannot apply for the same job?

Point 17:

2.09, 8.07 and 8.08 would fit in the new "Self"
