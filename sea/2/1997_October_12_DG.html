Software Engineering Code of Ethics, Version 3.0 Email from David LaRue to Gotterbarn: "Software Engineering Code of Ethics, Version 3.0 - comments)" - October 12, 1997 (Level 2) E-mail

Mr. Gotterbarn,

I generally favor Version 3 of the Software Engineering Code of Ethics as published in the October 1997 issue of Computer magazine.

I have some trouble with the wording of 3.07, Avoiding conflicting financial interests. As worded it seems overly broad in one sense and to narrow in another. I realize that it is just as important to avoid an impression of conflict of interest as it is a real conflict of interest. However, being engineers we are sometimes called upon by all concerned to render an opinion or vote on a subject. Need we avoid giving opinions when we feel that we can be fair and impartial to all
concerned? I marked 3.07 as uncertain.

For instance, current cryptographic standards are formulated by those who have an in depth knowledge of the subject. This is at least true for IEEE-P1363. These persons make policy based on what a standard should contain and what is correct. These persons may also certainly
represent companies who have a vested interest in such standards. They are also experts in the field; they should have some say.

I also believe that mentioning "governmental and professonal body" is too narrow. It seems to leave a loophole for company based activities. It seems that the words "Avoiding conflicting financial interests" is a better way of stating the concept.

For example, while I was contracting to a computer manufacturer, that manufacturer was asked to co-develop a product for a health care provider. Our allegance fell amoung several employers, customers, and users. However, it was possible to render opinions on equiptment and
standards to each company involved without feeling bias for the companies who were not important at the time. Our group was placed into a trusted position and all participants knew of the relationship. This sort of conflict may easily result when a single employer and product relationship is not established. This is often the norm these days.

Perhaps 3.07 could reference 3.06 to help expose conflicts to all concerned. If approved, an opinion could still be rendered.

Perhaps 3.07 could be more like the simple four word definition.

Thanks,
David LaRue
ACM, IEEE-CS
