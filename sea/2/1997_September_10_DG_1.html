Author: Don Gotterbarn
Date: September 10, 1997
Subject: Email from Mechler to Ilene Burnstein: "Forward of Miller's changes to Informed Consent and the Computing Professional - 7/16/96"
Recepient: Dennis Frailey
Format: Format
Source: Don Gotterbarn

Re: Meeting of Executive Committee Email from Mechler to Ilene Burnstein: "Forward of Miller's changes to Informed Consent and the Computing Professional - 7/16/96" - September 10, 1997 (Level 2) E-mail

Dennis,

Your response raises several concerns.

1. In February we sent a project plan with a goal of completing the Code by December and having it recommended by the Steering committee. We also advocated the need to then continue the development and propogation of the Code by developing supporting materials. This need to support Codes has been recognized by both societies. IEEE has its Ethics committee and ACM has its Committee on Professional Ethics to do this. From your coments it seems as if the Steering Committee has no intent to follow through on this which is both surprising and disappointing.

You had earlier indicated that I would get to meet with the Steering Committee to discuss the concerns of the SEEPP task force and future plans. It would be useful to talk to the committee. I will be in Pittsburgh at the IEEE Frontiers in Education Conference November 5-8 and could meet with some of the committee then. It would be an ideal opportunity to finalize the critical stages of this vital project.

2. As I indicated in the status report, we are polling the membership of IEEE-CS and ACM about the Code and will have the results by mid-November. Using these results the Code will be modified. A report on the results and a final version of the Code which takes into account these responses will be forwarded to the Steering Committee for recommendation. With a concerted effort, our task force can get this material ready by mid-December. This plan is consistent with the schedule we sent to the steering committee in February. In your response to me you did not indicate the date of the last meeting of the Steering Committee, but only that they want to finish their work by years-end.

3. In the original budget I sent to you, the only funding item approved was travel for the executive committee to meet and do the final reworking of the Code (see item 2 above). We have been working with a $0 budget from the Societies for the last year. The effort has been funded by our employers and the task force members. This amounts to a significant sum of money donated to the project. Given the time constraint of the end-of-year termination of the Steering Committee it is imperative that the SEEPP executive committee get together to adequately address the issues that will arise from the members responses. There will not be time to revist each issue multiple times after the polling results are obtained. In the light of the results from the polling we can also better formulate a plan for the propogation of the Software Engineering Code of Ethics. In order to hit the end of the December deadline the SEEPP Executive Committee has to meet and produce the final document. Hence the need for financial support at this critical stage.

As you said in your note, if it is not done by the end of December it will "go into the pile of incomplete work". Given the amount of work that has already gone into the development and improvement of the Code and the International recognition it has already achieved, I hope the Steering Committee can adequately support the effort to complete the code.

I would appreciate an early response so that we can schedule in time to complete the taskes. You will recall that Simon Rogerson is already under pressure from his university to finalize his visit to the US for this project.

thanks

don
