Gene and Leonard,

I agree with Leonard that these principles look very familiar (I taught Computer Ethics in our CS program (2 hours, required course.) But I think it is a very good integration of all ethics principles. My trouble in reading this and understanding it completely is primarily due to the so?called three?level classification of clauses: Aspire, Expect and Demand. I cannot tell (with ease) which is which. For example, 3.03. Reject bribery. Is it "Aspire" (to be human???) or Expect (to be professional) or Demand (good practice)? Not sure if all human "like" to reject bribery by the very human nature. We were "taught" through our education not to take bribery in this society. It would be a good service to us (educators) to label each clause one of the three levels if our dear authors can sort them out clearly. There are still some typos I guess.

Otherwise I am excited about seeing it and would love to use it in my course.

Regards,

---Carl
