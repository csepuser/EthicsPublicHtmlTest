Searching the NanoEthicsBank

 

The NanoEthicsBank is a database conceived as a resource for researchers, scholars, students, and the general public who are interested in the social and ethical implications of nanotechnology. Items in the database include normative documents, such as guidelines for safety in the workplace, and descriptive materials, such as analysis of the U.S. government’s capacity for oversight and studies of the media coverage of nanotechnology.

 

In some instances, the NEB is unable to provide full text access to materials included in the database because of copyright restrictions. Your local academic or public library may be able to provide access to publications cited in this database. IIT users can access most journal and newspaper articles through Galvin Library’s electronic resources. If you have any questions, please ask the CSEP librarian for assistance.

 

 The NEB is similar to other article databases you may have used through an academic or public library. It is an annotated bibliography of materials that can be searched using keywords, by subject terms that an indexer has used to classify each entry, or by advanced or Boolean searching. 

 

Unlike conventional article databases, the NEB has included another way of searching the archive. As an experiment, we are asking frequent users of the database to add their own words or “tags” to records. For example, a researcher may find an article on the medical use of nanotechnology and add the word “nanomedicine” to the record. Now, a search of the word “nanomedicine” will bring up this article.  Please see the User Tag page for more information and how to become a contributor.

 

Basic Search

 

Does a basic search of the Title, Author, Publisher, Subject, User tags, and abstract fields. Enter your search terms in the Search field and click the Search button. 

 

Advanced Search

 

Allows a user to specify what fields in the record will be searched.  Use this option when you are looking for a specific author, title, or publisher.  The NEB also allows you to search using the subject drop-down menu.  User Tags -words or phrases added by other users of the database to describe material- can also be searched in this mode.

Boolean Search

 

The NEB can also be searched using the symbols, ‘+,’ ‘-,’ ‘~,’ spaces, quotation marks, and truncation symbols to create a Boolean search equation. Simply change the type of search from Normal to Boolean in the drop-down box below the Search field. For information on how to perform a Boolean search, see the help page.

 

AutoSuggest

 

Auto Suggest is the new feature included in the NEB Search. It porvides a suggestion based on the term user enters in the Search field.When user clicks on one of the topics in the drop-down list, the search term would be automatically filled with the topic User wants to search.
